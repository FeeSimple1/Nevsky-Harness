NEVSKY: TEUTONS AND RUS IN COLLISION, 1240-1242
STRATEGY RECOMMENDATIONS (Second Edition)
================================================================
 
Sources: GMT Playbook (Albright/Wagner play hints, Ruhnke design notes),
Second Edition Rules of Play, Second Edition Changes Summary, project map
reference, Volko Ruhnke's "Nevsky: Scenario Balance and Strategy" on
InsideGMT, BGG threads, and Stuart Ellis-Gorman's reviews. Where the
Rules and the Scenario Adjustments / Errata diverge, the latter govern.
 
The "Book of Strategy" attributed to the Byzantine Emperor Maurice
(c. 600 AD) frames the whole game: hurt the enemy by deceit, raids,
or hunger; never be enticed into a pitched battle, which depends more
on luck than bravery. Nevsky rewards exactly that mindset. The
chronicles record only four field battles in the entire 1240-1242
conflict. Plan for ravage, siege, and starvation; treat battle as a
closing move or a desperate one.
 
 
================================================================
I. CORE PRINCIPLES THAT GOVERN EVERY DECISION
================================================================
 
1. THE CALENDAR IS THE REAL OPPONENT.
   Every Lord's Service marker is a doomsday clock. The game ends not
   when one side runs out of soldiers but when one side runs out of
   willing Lords. Work backward from each Lord's Disband box and ask:
   what do I get out of him before he goes home? A Lord who Disbands
   in a Friendly Locale with Coin or Loot to spare was wasted; a Lord
   who Disbands at the end of a successful sack was used correctly.
 
2. LOGISTICS DICTATE GEOGRAPHY.
   Forces and Assets exist on Lord mats; what you can do with them
   exists on the map. The bridge between the two is Transport, which
   only works in certain Seasons:
     - Sleds: Winter only, all Ways.
     - Carts: Summer only, Trackways only.
     - Boats: Rasputitsa and Summer, Waterways only.
     - Ships: Rasputitsa and Summer, Seaports only (and Novgorod for
       Russians).
   Rasputitsa is the most restrictive: no Trackway transport at all
   (Sleds gone, Carts not yet usable). Anything you want to ship over
   Trackways in mud has to walk, which limits Provender to the amount
   the Lord can carry on his back.
 
3. PROVENDER IS THE CURRENCY OF MOVEMENT.
   A Lord with 6 or fewer units consumes 1 Provender per Move/Fight;
   7+ units consumes 2. So that seventh Vassal you Levied "for the
   muscle" is a permanent supply tax. Only call up the units you need
   for the mission ahead. Hermann or Aleksandr maxed out is a fine
   storming hammer for one campaign and an unfeedable mob the next.
 
4. THE FRIENDLY LOCALE TEST.
   At the END of every Campaign, ask of each of your Lords: where will
   he be standing, and is it Friendly to him? Friendly means his side's
   home territory or a Stronghold his side has Conquered. Lords need
   Friendly ground to:
     - Muster (add Capabilities, Levy Vassals, Levy Transport).
     - Pay with Loot (Coin works at the same Locale anywhere; Loot
       does not).
     - Forage outside Summer (Friendly Stronghold required in Winter
       and Rasputitsa).
   Russian Veche Coin is the great exception: it can shift any
   non-Besieged Russian Lord's Service from afar. Treat that
   exception as the Russians' single biggest flexibility lever.
 
5. PAY > DISBAND.
   Loot is easier to obtain than Coin (especially for Teutons), so
   plan campaigns that end with Lords in Friendly Locales holding Loot.
   Coin can travel between co-located Lords, including via Sharing.
   Veche Coin can travel anywhere. Don't hoard Coin; spend it on the
   Lord whose Service is up first, and ravage to refill.
 
6. RAVAGE IS NOT FREE ANYMORE (2ND EDITION).
   Under the Second Edition, Ravage costs TWO actions if an Unbesieged
   enemy Lord is adjacent. That changes the calculus on raids in
   contested zones: you cannot hit-and-run while a defender stares at
   you across one Way. Force the defender to leave first, or
   accompany your raider with a screening Lord.
 
7. RAVAGE ALSO DECAYS (2ND EDITION).
   At end of each Rasputitsa (turns 8 and 16), the side whose
   territory was Ravaged removes Ravaged markers down to half their
   number, rounded up. So Ravage VP earned in the first half-year of
   either campaign year erodes if you don't keep ravaging. Ravage is
   tempo, not a savings account.
 
8. STORM IS CAPPED (2ND EDITION).
   Storm Melee is now capped at 6 Hits per Lord per Round. Big stacks
   of Knights still wreck a Fort, but a single super-Lord cannot one-
   shot a City the way he sometimes did under First Edition. Think in
   terms of multiple Lords for big walls, or sieges + waiting.
 
9. WHEN DRAWN AS EVENTS, "NO" CARDS ARE GONE FOREVER (2ND EDITION).
   Each side has three "No Event/No Capability" cards. Drawing one as
   an Event removes it from play. The Event deck thins and your odds
   of useful draws improve over the game. Plan for the late game to
   be more Event-rich than the early game. Pleskau scenario removes
   them at the start; the full Crusade on Novgorod scenario keeps them.
 
 
================================================================
II. THE LEVY PHASE - WHAT TO BUILD
================================================================
 
Levy is where most games are won or lost. Spend Lordship actions
deliberately. The hierarchy of value is roughly:
 
Capability cards >= Vassals (for combat power) > Transport > extra
Forces from already-Mustered Vassals.
 
CAPABILITIES YOU SHOULD ALMOST ALWAYS WANT
 
TEUTONS:
  - WILLIAM OF MODENA. Brings the Legate into play. Adds a Command
    action when Co-located with a Lord on Campaign, allows Mustering
    a Lord without rolling Fealty, and provides a free Lord shift
    during Call to Arms. The drawback: enemy Lords can endanger and
    discard the Legate. Worth it in nearly every scenario long enough
    to use him more than once. Cost is the Grand Prince Event being
    out of play (which would have delayed Aleksandr/Andrey).
  - TREATY OF STENSBY. +1 Command for both Heinrich and Knud & Abel.
    Excellent if either Dane is in play. The trade-off is removing
    the Grand Prince Event (which is also taken out by William of
    Modena, so doubling up is no loss).
  - ORDENSBURGEN. Turns Commanderies into extra Seats and grants
    +1 Command when a Teutonic Lord starts at one. Powerful with
    Andreas (Wenden) and any Dorpat-based push.
  - HALBBRUEDER (T9/T10). Sergeants AND Men-at-Arms get Armor +1.
    Pin this on Hermann or Andreas in any scenario where they expect
    to fight. Routs only - doesn't help Loss rolls.
  - WARRIOR MONKS (T7/T15). One Knights re-roll per Archery and
    Melee step. Stack with Halbbrueder on your hammer Lord.
  - BALISTARII Crossbowmen (T4-T6). Gives that Lord's Men-at-Arms
    Archery with -2 enemy Armor. The only way Teutons get Battle
    Archery in the open field. Vital if you expect to defend.
  - HILLFORTS OF THE SWORD BRETHREN (T8). Each Feed skips one
    Unbesieged Teutonic Lord in Livonia. Free meals while consolidating.
  - COGS (T18). Big Sail capacity for Knud & Abel and Heinrich, plus
    blocks Russian Baltic Sea Trade. A nasty combo if you can keep
    the Danes operating.
 
RUSSIANS:
  - BLACK SEA TRADE (R8). 1 Coin per Call to Arms unless Lovat or
    Novgorod is Conquered. Hard for the Teutons to disrupt. The
    Playbook recommends taking it almost every game and that is
    correct - Coin is the Russians' problem and this directly fixes
    it. Better than Baltic Sea Trade because Baltic depends on ship
    counts and the Neva.
  - ARCHBISHOPRIC OF NOVGOROD (R15). Novgorod becomes a Seat for
    every Russian Lord, plus +1 Command starting from Novgorod.
    Stacks brilliantly with the Volkhov-Ladoga waterway spine for
    Supply.
  - LUCHNIKI (R1/R2). Light Horse and Militia get Archery. Combined
    with Streltsy, the Russians can deliver brutal first-Round
    Archery before the Teutons close to Melee.
  - DRUZHINA (R5/R6). +1 Command and Defending Round 1-2 Archery
    not halved. A Knights piece on the mat is the only requirement.
  - STEPPE WARRIORS (R10). Lets Aleksandr and Andrey muster Asiatic
    Horse - mounted archers with Evade. Great in Battle, fragile in
    Storm. Disbands if discarded, so don't drop the card carelessly.
  - STONE KREMLIN. Gives one Lord Walls +1 to place at Strongholds.
    Park it on Domash and use the actions to fortify Novgorod, Pskov,
    Ladoga over multiple turns. Discard later for two This-Lord
    Capabilities.
  - SMERDI (R4). Pool of Serfs that can be re-Mustered each campaign,
    even after losses. Cheap arrow-fodder for big battles or storms.
  - LODYA (R16). Russian Boats count as Ships for blockade math.
    Worth pairing with Black Sea Trade or as a hedge against Cogs.
  - RAIDERS (R12/R14) - Russian variant. A mounted Lord can ravage
    one adjacent unoccupied Locale per Command card. Dramatically
    extends the reach of Domash, Karelians, or Andrey for VP.
 
CAPABILITIES TO PASS ON OR DEFER
 
  - "This Lord" Capabilities pinned on a Lord who is about to Disband
    are wasted; they are discarded when he leaves.
  - Capabilities matched to Lords not yet on the map are speculative;
    if your scenario is short, only buy them when the Lord arrives.
  - Crusade on Novgorod (T11) Summer Crusaders disband each Late Winter -
    plan to use them Summer, NOT into a Winter siege.
 
EVENT VS CAPABILITY TENSION
Picking up a Capability removes its Event from the deck, AND prevents
the same card's Event from being drawn against you. Sometimes the
defensive purpose alone justifies the buy: e.g., Russians taking
Baltic Sea Trade may be more about the Coin, but Teutons taking
Treaty of Stensby keeps the awkward Event from biting them. Always
read both halves before deciding.
 
 
================================================================
III. CALL TO ARMS - PAPAL LEGATE AND THE VECHE
================================================================
 
LEGATE (Teutonic, only when William of Modena is in play):
  - Place him at any Bishopric. The four Teutonic Bishoprics (Riga,
    Dorpat, Leal, Reval) cover the whole map; pick the one closest to
    where you need an extra action this campaign.
  - Use him to Muster a Ready Lord without a Fealty roll. Save this
    for unreliable Lords (low Fealty) or critical timing.
  - Use him to bump a Lord's Calendar marker by one box left.
  - Use him to give a Lord a free extra Muster at full Lordship.
    This is huge on a Lord at a Friendly Locale with a long Service.
  - PROTECT HIM. He's discarded if he's with a Lord who Avoids
    Battle, Withdraws, Retreats, or is left alone with Russians.
    Don't strap him to your raiding Lord; he belongs on the Lord
    you intend to stand with.
 
VECHE (Russian):
  - Each conquered VP marker spent shifts an Aleksandr/Andrey
    cylinder LEFT 2 boxes (was 1 in 1st Edition). That's the bigger
    Russian lever now.
  - Or auto-Muster a Ready Russian Lord (no roll).
  - Or grant a Russian Lord an immediate extra Muster at full
    Lordship.
  - Decline a Ready Aleksandr or Andrey to gain 1 VP.
 
THE ALEKSANDR/ANDREY DECISION (THE CORE RUSSIAN PUZZLE):
  - Bringing them in: they are the strongest Russian Lords with the
    biggest forces and best Command. Aleksandr is always Marshal
    when on map.
  - Declining them: each decline is +1 VP into the Veche box, which
    can later pay any Russian Lord on or off the front.
  - Rule of thumb: decline early when the front is quiet, then bring
    them in when the Teutons threaten Novgorod or you need a hammer.
    Don't simply hoard VP; declined VP is Coin-to-be, not score on
    the calendar - and Veche box is capped at 8.
  - In short scenarios (Pleskau, Watland), the prince in question
    should usually come out only if you have a clear use; the
    longer the scenario, the more declining-then-spending pays off.
 
 
================================================================
IV. PLAN AND COMMAND - THE STACK
================================================================
 
Plan (the face-down Command card stack) is the most underrated phase.
Two players stack simultaneously, then alternate flipping. You can
include up to as many Command cards as your Season allows: 6 in
Summer, 5 in Rasputitsa, 4 in Winter. Pad with Pass cards as needed.
 
PLANNING IDEAS:
 
1. PUT YOUR MOST FLEXIBLE LORD LATE.
   The card flipped last knows the most. Lords with high Command
   ratings (3 actions) like Aleksandr, Andreas, Hermann, Andrey are
   ideal late-stack pivots that can react to whatever happened.
 
2. PUT FORCED-MOVES EARLY.
   Lords whose Service is about to run out, who must reach a
   Friendly Locale, or who must sail before Rasputitsa, go FIRST
   while you still know the campaign has room.
 
3. PASS CARDS ARE NOT WASTED CARDS.
   A Pass let you do nothing while opponents commit. Stack them
   against the opponent's expected key actions. Conversely, fewer
   Pass cards mean you've Mustered more Lords - which is what you
   want.
 
4. LIEUTENANTS - THE FORCE-CONCENTRATION HACK.
   During Planning, a non-Marshal Lord can stack ON TOP of another
   non-Marshal Lord at the same Locale, becoming Lieutenant. They
   move together for the entire Campaign. The Lower Lord's Command
   card becomes a Pass when revealed. So two Lords with 2 Commands
   each, joined as Lieutenant + Lower, give you 2 actions on the
   Lieutenant's card and a free Pass on the Lower's. That's 2 active
   moves for two cards instead of 4 - usually worse, BUT it lets
   you operate two Lords as one army without needing a Marshal, and
   the Lower Lord's Forces add to combat. Effective when Hermann
   isn't a Marshal (because Andreas is on map), or when you need
   Yaroslav to follow Hermann around.
 
5. MARSHAL GROUP MARCH.
   Marshals (Aleksandr, Andreas, plus Andrey/Hermann if their
   primary Marshal isn't on map) can drag any same-side Unbesieged
   Lords at their Locale along on a March for free. This is the
   key to building a battle force: have your Lords physically
   converge during earlier campaigns, then let the Marshal march
   the whole stack.
 
 
================================================================
V. CAMPAIGN ACTIONS - DOING THINGS WITH WHAT YOU BUILT
================================================================
 
MARCH:
  - Movement is by Way (Trackway or Waterway), one Locale per Command.
    Marshal can take everyone. Lieutenant must take Lower Lord.
  - Laden March (more Provender than usable Transport, but no more
    than double) costs 2 actions per Locale. Loot ALWAYS makes you
    Laden regardless of Transport.
  - Avoid Battle and Retreat are Unladen-only - so if you're Laden
    and offered combat, you MUST fight or discard down. Plan an
    escape route before you stick your nose into enemy land.
 
SUPPLY (2ND EDITION):
  - Now requires 1 Transport per Provender per Way (no double-duty
    Transport). This is a real nerf to long supply lines.
  - From a Seat (or Archbishopric Novgorod for Russians, Commanderies
    for Teutons), 1 Provender per Source, max 2 Sources per action.
  - Plus 1 Provender per Ship at Seaport (Russians: at Novgorod), max
    2 Ships per action.
  - In Winter only Seats provide Supply (no Ships).
  - The further your Lord ranges, the more Sleds/Boats you need to
    bring food forward. Stockpile Transport during Levy.
 
FORAGE:
  - 1 Provender per action.
  - In Summer, anywhere not Ravaged.
  - In Winter/Rasputitsa, only at a Friendly Stronghold.
  - DO NOT Ravage the countryside around a Stronghold you intend to
    Conquer and Forage from later - the Playbook flags this as a
    common Hermann-deep-in-Rus problem.
 
RAVAGE:
  - Towns/Strongholds/Trade Routes: 1/2 VP + 1 Provender + 1 Loot.
  - Regions: 1/2 VP + 1 Provender (no Loot).
  - 2 actions if Unbesieged enemy Lord adjacent (2nd Ed).
  - 1 action otherwise.
  - Cannot Ravage a Locale already Ravaged or your own Locale.
  - Ravaged markers half-decay at end of each Rasputitsa, so build
    them up shortly before scenario end if going for a VP win.
 
SAIL:
  - Costs ALL actions on the card.
  - Rasputitsa or Summer only.
  - Requires 1 Ship per Horse unit (Teuton) or 2 Ships per Horse
    (Russian), plus 1 Ship per Provender, plus 2 per Loot.
  - Cannot Sail into a Locale with an Unbesieged enemy Lord.
  - Excellent for Danes (Knud & Abel) crossing the Baltic, less
    common for Russians.
 
TAX:
  - All actions on the card. 1 Coin to your mat, at your Seat only.
  - Yaroslav's Seat is Pskov when Conquered by Teutons, so a parked
    Yaroslav there generates Coin without leaving the walls.
  - Aleksandr at Novgorod (Archbishopric capability) is similar.
 
BESIEGE / STORM / SALLY:
  - Set down a Siege marker by entering an enemy Stronghold.
  - Each Siege action with enough Lords for Siege Capacity adds
    one more Siege marker, up to 4. Strongholds have Surrender odds
    equal to current Siege markers; sometimes terms beat storm.
  - Storm: Attacker generates Hits as in Battle, but Defender's
    Walls equal Stronghold Walls value. Garrison units defend first.
    Attackers Storm with Walls = Siege markers there.
  - Storm Melee is capped at 6 Hits per Lord per Round (2nd Ed).
  - Don't Storm a fully-staffed City unless you have the Lords
    and the time. Forts (Walls 1-3) are reasonable Storm targets;
    Castles (with Walls +1 from Stonemasons or starting marker) and
    Cities (Walls 1-4) are not, except as a last move.
  - Surrender via Siege gives no Spoils but removes the Lord and
    Conquers the Stronghold. Storm gives Loot/Provender/Coin = VP
    of the Stronghold and removes all losing Lords PERMANENTLY.
 
BATTLE:
  - Initiative order: Defending Archery, Attacking Archery, Defending
    Horse Melee, Attacking Horse Melee, Defending Foot Melee,
    Attacking Foot Melee.
  - In Pursuit (after one side Concedes), the conceding side's Hits
    are halved per step.
  - Concede at the START of the round, before Strikes. You give up
    the field but keep Forces and Assets. Conceding to a Pursuit is
    often correct: surviving with a Lord intact beats winning a
    Pyrrhic battle.
  - Retreat after a lost battle: Service gets a die roll to shift
    LEFT (half rounded up). Withdraw into a Stronghold instead if
    you can - no Service penalty, no Spoils to enemy.
 
KEY BATTLE EVENTS TO REMEMBER (Hold cards):
  Russian:
    - HILL (R hold) - Defending, Round 1-2 Archery is x1.
    - BRIDGE - opponent's front center Lord can Strike with limited
      units in early rounds (devastating in non-Winter).
    - MARSH - Defending, non-Winter, Russian Horse blocks enemy
      Horse Strikes for two Rounds (Rus side calls it differently).
    - AMBUSH - blocks Avoid Battle or ignores enemy left/right in
      Round 1.
  Teutonic:
    - HILL (T hold) - Defending, Round 1-2 Teutonic Archery is x1.
    - FIELD ORGAN - Round 1, his Knights AND Sergeants Melee
      Strike +1 (huge first-round shock). Stack on Andreas/Hermann.
 
If you draw multiple Hold Events, keep them under your mat and play
on the right Lord at the right moment. They are why the second half
of a campaign is more dangerous than the first - and the second half
of the game more dangerous than the first, since "No" cards exit
permanently after being drawn.

VI. SIDES - HOW EACH ONE WANTS TO WIN
================================================================
 
THE TEUTONS - INVADER WITH A CLOCK
 
Comparative advantages:
  - Knights (4-Armor heavy cavalry) are the best raw combat unit.
  - Multiple Bishoprics (2 VP each) and a Legate that uses them.
  - Halbbrueder + Warrior Monks combos make a single Lord nearly
    unstoppable in the field.
  - Hermann starts forward (Dorpat) with usable units.
  - Sea power via Knud & Abel, Heinrich (Cogs).
 
Comparative disadvantages:
  - Most Lords' Service is short or fragile (Knud & Abel get pulled
    by Khan Baty event; Andreas drawn off by Osilian Revolt).
  - Few archers in field battle without Balistarii Capability.
  - Ravage in Rus is fine for VP but the countryside is sparse.
  - Andreas and Rudolf, if removed, are out for many turns - more
    so than Karelians/Yaroslav for the Russians.
 
Strategic plan:
  - Win by Conquered Strongholds (1 or 2 VP each + sustained
    Friendly base for further operations) and by Ravage VP in Rus.
  - Pskov (2 VP) and Novgorod (3 VP) are the trophies; Izborsk
    Fort (1 VP) is the doorstep and is the realistic Pleskau target.
  - Use Hermann as the army-builder out of Dorpat. Andreas, if/when
    Mustered, anchors Livonia and can hammer Vod.
  - Keep the Danes (Knud & Abel, Heinrich) on the coast or sailing.
    They can Conquer Koporye/Kaibolovo and pin Vladislav from the
    sea. Threat alone often suffices.
  - Maintain a Friendly base IN Rus once you Conquer a Fort, so
    you can re-Muster, re-Levy, and Forage.
  - Watch your Lord placement at the end of each Campaign. Hermann
    sitting outside an unconquered Fort with his Service expiring
    is the signature Teutonic blunder.
 
THE RUSSIANS - DEFENDER WITH A LATE GAME
 
Comparative advantages:
  - Veche Coin can pay any Lord anywhere (huge logistical flexibility).
  - Archery edge (Luchniki + Streltsy + horse archers via Steppe
    Warriors).
  - Aleksandr (Marshal) and Andrey, when summoned, are top-tier.
  - The Volkhov-Ladoga-Novgorod-Rusa-Lovat waterway spine plus
    Archbishopric Capability gives the best supply network in the
    game.
  - Black Sea Trade Capability is hard to disrupt.
 
Comparative disadvantages:
  - Starts the game with very few Lords on map. Early game can be
    a crisis if Hermann moves fast.
  - No archery in field battle without Capability cards.
  - Aleksandr/Andrey are slow to arrive without spending VP.
  - Small Russian Forts have Walls 1-3 and small Garrisons - Teutons
    can Storm them.
 
Strategic plan:
  - Survive the opening, then counter-punch.
  - Take Black Sea Trade at first opportunity. Take Archbishopric
    when Novgorod is your supply hub.
  - Use the Veche to either decline princes for VP early (when the
    front is quiet) or summon them when needed.
  - Drag the Teutons toward unfavorable Sieges that exhaust their
    Service. Yaroslav at Pskov, if unsupported, will eventually
    Disband on his own from short Service - keep him under Siege so
    he can't Tax.
  - Don't fight pitched battles you don't have to. Russian defenders
    with Hill, Druzhina, and a fortified position are very tough;
    Russians attacking Knights in the open are not.
  - Aleksandr at the Neva or Vod with sufficient Light Horse and
    Streltsy is the historical Battle of the Ice setup; it works in
    the game too.

VII. SCENARIO-SPECIFIC NOTES
================================================================
 
PLESKAU (1240, 2 turns) - Teaching scenario, Teuton offensive.
 
  Per Volko Ruhnke on InsideGMT: balanced under optimal play.
  Hermann should take Izborsk turn 1 by STORM to sack it (sieging to
  surrender denies Spoils). The conquered Izborsk becomes Friendly,
  letting the Teutons Levy and Muster from there for the second
  Levy. The Teutons need not march on Pskov at all - if Gavrilo
  stands at Pskov, ravage the countryside (1/2 VP a pop) instead.
  Special victory condition: each Lord Disband or removal = 1 VP.
  Watch Vladislav of Ladoga; he can ravage Estonia. Keep the Danes
  visible at Reval/Narwia to deter him. (2nd Ed: "No" cards removed
  before play, so Event deck is dense.)
 
WATLAND (1241, up to 5 turns) - Teutonic invasion of Vod.
 
  Volko's own retrospective acknowledges this scenario favors the
  Teutons. The Russians realistically can't push back before
  Rasputitsa shuts down trackway movement. The 2nd Ed special rule
  raises the Teutonic threshold (need 7 VP AND double Russian VP),
  which helps.
  Russian focus: spend Veche VP early to bring Andrey on as soon as
  possible (the only scenario that really showcases the Aleksandr/
  Andrey decision in isolation), build Black Sea Trade Coin, hold
  Novgorod and the Volkhov spine, and use Vladislav for Estonian
  raids while Hermann/Andreas are committed in Vod.
  Teutonic focus: Andreas at Fellin should drive on Koporye. Knud &
  Abel sail to Wesenberg/Reval and threaten down the coast. Yaroslav
  at Pskov holds the south.
 
PEIPUS (1242, up to 4 turns) - Russian counter-offensive.
 
  The scenario starts with 4 VP Conquered for Russians and 3 Coin
  in the Veche. Aleksandr, Andrey, Domash, Karelians all start at
  Novgorod. The Russians MUST move fast - 2 Winter turns then 2
  Rasputitsa turns where Trackways die.
  Volko's advice: don't fixate on Pskov. Yaroslav alone at Pskov
  will Disband on his own from short Service if you SIEGE him so
  he cannot Tax. Pair this with Ransom Capability (R7) for Coin.
  Alternative: ravage past Pskov (Izborsk if free, Ostrov, Velikaya
  River) and conquer undefended Teutonic Castles before Rasputitsa.
  Levy Raiders Capability for extra ravaging reach. Levy Boats
  before Rasputitsa hits because Sleds/Carts become useless.
  Ships + Lodya make Baltic Sea Trade work post-Winter. Spend Veche
  Coin liberally - it pays Lords anywhere, mud or no mud.
 
RETURN OF THE PRINCE (1241-1242, up to 8 turns).
 
  Longer Russian counter-campaign. Aleksandr starts at Novgorod;
  Andreas at Koporye. The Russians must reverse Vodian losses and
  ideally wreck Teutonic VP before time runs out. Black Sea Trade
  early. Stone Kremlin on Domash to harden Novgorod and Ladoga.
  Plan a winter campaign to retake Koporye while Sleds are usable.
 
NICOLLE VARIANT - alternate Return of the Prince setup based on
  David Nicolle's interpretation. Strategically similar but with
  Hermann at Dorpat instead of out of play, expanding Teutonic
  options southward.
 
CRUSADE ON NOVGOROD (1240-1242, full 16-turn).
 
  The biggest scenario, and the one where the Disband/re-Muster
  cycle pays off. Lords like the Karelians and Rudolf turn over
  fast - that's fine, they re-enter. Andreas and Andrey, if they
  leave, are gone for a long time. Don't let those two be wasted.
  "No" cards are NOT removed when drawn here, so the event deck
  stays slow throughout - a longer game of measured tempo.
  Teutons: the early campaigns are yours. Pskov in 1240, Watland
  in 1241. Stack VP early because Aleksandr is coming.
  Russians: tough early. Veche VP, decline princes, Black Sea Trade,
  build Coin. Bring Aleksandr in around the start of 1241 once Vod
  is in danger; bring Andrey in by winter 1241/42 for the Peipus
  campaign. The game IS called Nevsky for a reason.

VIII. THE TWENTY MOST USEFUL HABITS
================================================================
 
1. Read both halves of every Capability card before you Levy it.
   You're forfeiting an Event to gain a Capability.
 
2. Never Muster a Vassal that takes you over 6 units unless this
   campaign clearly justifies the doubled Provender cost.
 
3. End every Campaign asking: "Is each of my Lords on Friendly
   ground? Does each who needs to Pay have Loot or Coin?"
 
4. Loot is for Friendly Locales (or for Feeding anywhere). If you're
   raiding, plan a route home before you start.
 
5. Discard surplus Provender to Avoid Battle - don't get caught
   Laden by a fast-moving enemy.
 
6. Don't Ravage the immediate hinterland of a Fort you intend to
   capture and Forage from. (Hermann at Pskov problem.)
 
7. Concede the field early if a battle is going badly. Pursuit
   halves Hits and you keep your Lord and Forces.
 
8. Withdraw into a friendly Stronghold instead of Retreating across
   the open. Same outcome, no Service penalty.
 
9. Lieutenants are an underused tool when you have two slow Lords
   in the same place and no Marshal to share with.
 
10. Plan Command stacks with reaction Lords late: Aleksandr,
    Andreas, Hermann, Andrey want to flip last.
 
11. Use the Legate on Lords with low Fealty; spare your Lordship
    actions for Capabilities and Vassals.
 
12. Spend Veche Coin proactively. Hoarded Veche Coin caps at 8 -
    everything past that is wasted.
 
13. Decline Aleksandr/Andrey early, then summon when needed. Don't
    flip the order.
 
14. Take Black Sea Trade and Archbishopric for Russians; William of
    Modena for Teutons. These three bend the system.
 
15. Storm small Russian Forts with mass Knights; siege Cities and
    Bishoprics or pass them by.
 
16. Carry one or two Hold Events into Battle - they swing close
    fights. Hill, Bridge, Marsh, Field Organ, Ambush.
 
17. In Rasputitsa, don't be deep in enemy territory across
    Trackways unless you have Provender on legs (Loot) and an
    escape via Waterway.
 
18. Re-evaluate your Capability cards each Campaign. Discard
    "This Lord" cards that have lost their target rather than let
    them clog up your two-Capability cap.
 
19. Track your opponent's Service markers; one Lord Disbanding is
    often worth 2-3 VP of pressure to engineer.
 
20. Remember Maurice's dictum. Hurt the enemy with hunger and
    raids. Battle is the last resort, not the first.
 
End of strategy notes.
