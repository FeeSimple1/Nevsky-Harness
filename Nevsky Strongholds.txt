Nevsky — Strongholds Reference
Sources: Rules of Play 2nd Edition (1.3.1, 4.5.1, 4.5.2); Second Edition Changes Summary; Foldout PAC Strongholds table.
Conquest vs. Sack: A Stronghold becomes Conquered either by Surrender (Siege roll ≤ Siege markers, no Lord inside) or by Sack (won by Storm). Both yield the VP. Only Sack yields Loot, Provender, and Coin equal to the VP value (4.5.1 Terms; 4.5.2 Sack).

Notes

Surrender vs. Sack matters strategically. A long Siege that ends in Surrender hands the besieger VP but no assets — and any inside Lord goes through normal Disband, providing Coin via Ransom. A Storm risks units and Lords but pays the assets if it succeeds.
Sack also permanently removes any Besieged Lord(s) inside (1.5.1) — Surrender does not.
Novgorod is the only Archbishopric, and only via Aleksandr's R15 Archbishopric of Novgorod Capability (which adds it as his Seat). The Stronghold itself is always treated as a Novgorod-row entry on the table.
Trade Routes are boxed Locales: they take a Conquered marker for 1 VP but have no Walls, Capacity, or Garrison. They flip simply by an enemy Lord's presence with no friendly Lord contesting — no Storm involved, hence no Spoils.
Walls are rolled only in Storm, Sally, or by specific Event — not in normal Battle (4.4.2, 4.5.2).
Capacity governs Siegeworks: a Besieging army with Lords ≥ Capacity may add a Siege marker per Siege action, up to four. Below Capacity, the Besiegers can only starve the defenders out.
Garrison hits absorbed first. In Storm, the Defender must assign Hits to Garrison units until they Rout, before any Defending Lord's units take Hits.
Garrisons are independent of the Defending Lord's cards. Halbbrüder does not give Garrison MaA Armor +1; Crossbowmen does not stack on the Garrison's built-in –2 Armor effect. A Besieged Lord's Balistarii gives his units (not the Garrison) Archery.
Stone Kremlin (R18) can mark a Russian Fort, City, or Novgorod with Walls +1 (becoming Walls 1–4). Stonemasons (T17) can convert a Fort into a Castle (Walls 1–4 with Castle garrison/spoils), removing any Walls +1 marker there.
Storm Melee cap: each Lord per side adds at most 6 Melee Hits per Round; Archery is unlimited (4.5.2, 2nd Ed change).

Difference from input

The Second Edition Changes Summary reduced Bishopric and Castle garrisons by one Men-at-Arms each, so Bishopric 2 MaA + 1 Knight and Castle 1 MaA + 1 Knight supersede the original PAC printing.
Loot/Provender/Coin moved into a separate "Spoils if Sacked" column to reflect that they are awarded only on Storm, not on Siege Surrender.
