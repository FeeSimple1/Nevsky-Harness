NEVSKY MISCELLANEOUS RULES — Reference
=======================================
Compiled per Rules of Play (Second Edition, 2023). Sources cited
inline by section number. This document consolidates rules that
are referenced throughout the other reference files but do not
otherwise have a dedicated home — Asset rules, Locale mechanics,
the Papal Legate, Marker definitions, general setup, and a
glossary of key terms.
 
 
================================================================
PART I — ASSETS  (Section 1.7)
================================================================
 
ASSET TYPES:
  - Transport ... Boat, Cart, Sled, Ship.
                  Helps move Provender (Supply, March) and
                  enables Sail. Lords themselves can move
                  without Transport.
  - Provender ... Food and supplies. Consumed when Lords Feed
                  after Marching/Fighting, and to enable Laden
                  movement.
  - Coin ........ Money. Used to Pay (extend Service).
                  Carried on Lord mats AND held in the Veche
                  box (1.3.3, 1.4.2).
  - Loot ........ Livestock and other booty. Used to Pay (at
                  Friendly Locales) or to Feed (anywhere).
                  Acquired by Ravage and as Sack Spoils.
 
Asset markers are placed on Lord mats. Plain markers represent
1 each; "x2", "x3", "x4" markers represent those amounts (1.7.1).
 
ACCOUNTING (1.7.1):
  Lords may add and expend each type of Asset as if money,
  making change as needed. Veche Coin functions the same way.
 
GREED (1.7.2):
  A Lord may DISCARD Assets (as opposed to USE them) ONLY when
  the discard is needed to enable one of these movement actions:
    - March Laden       (4.3.2)
    - March Unladen     (4.3.2)
    - Avoid Battle      (4.3.4)
    - Retreat           (4.4.3)
    - Sail              (4.7.3)
  Outside these triggers, a Lord may not discard Assets.
  EXAMPLE: A Lord with surplus Provender at a Friendly Locale
  cannot simply "drop" it; the only way to lose it voluntarily
  is to expend it (Feed, Laden modifier) or via Wastage (4.9.4).
 
WASTAGE — PER LORD (1.7.3):
  Each Lord's mat may hold AT MOST 8 of each Asset type
  (8 Sleds, 8 Boats, 8 Carts, 8 Ships, 8 Provender, 8 Coin,
  8 Loot). Any excess gained beyond 8 is lost immediately.
 
WASTAGE — VECHE BOX (1.4.2):
  The Veche box holds AT MOST 8 Coin and 8 Conquered (1VP)
  markers. Any excess is lost.
 
WASTAGE — END OF CAMPAIGN (4.9.4):
  At end of each Campaign, Teutons then Russians: each Mustered
  Lord with MORE THAN ONE of any single Asset type, OR more
  than one This Lord Capability card, must DISCARD exactly one
  Asset or one such card from that Lord. (Owning player chooses
  which.) Wastage applies to LORDS only — not to Veche.
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
TRANSPORT — Usability Matrix  (Rule 1.7.4)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Transport is usable for March, Avoid Battle, Retreat, Supply,
and Sail. Each Transport type is usable only in certain Seasons
and along certain Way types:
 
  Type    Seasons              Where Usable
  ------  -------------------  -----------------------------------
  Boat    Rasputitsa, Summer   Waterways
  Ship    Rasputitsa, Summer   Seaports (Sail). Russians: also
                               Novgorod for Supply (4.6.3).
                               REMINDER: the Baltic Sea is NOT
                               a Waterway; cross it only by Sail.
  Sled    Early Winter,        Any Way (Trackway OR Waterway)
          Late Winter
  Cart    Summer               Trackways only
 
CONVERSION (4.9.3 — Plow & Reap):
  At end of the second Summer 40 Days, all Lords flip Carts to
  Sleds, then halve (round up).
  At end of the second Late Winter 40 Days, all Lords flip Sleds
  to Carts, then halve (round up).
  Unusable Transport otherwise stays with its Lord for future use.

PAY — Coin and Loot Restrictions  (Rules 3.2.1, 3.2.2)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Coin and Loot may ONLY be removed during the Pay step (Levy
3.2 or Campaign 4.8.2) so as to actually shift Service markers.
Each marker spent shifts a single Service marker 1 box right.
 
PAY WITH COIN (3.2.1):
  A Lord may spend Coin from his own mat, OR from the Veche
  box (Russians only), to shift Service of:
    - The paying Lord himself, OR
    - Another Lord at the same Locale, OR
    - (Veche Coin only) Any one Russian Lord who is NOT
      Besieged.
  Coin may be spent from any location it sits on (Lord mat or
  Veche). Each Coin = 1 box right.
  IMPORTANT: The only Coin that can affect a Besieged Lord is
  his own or that of another Lord Besieged with him.
 
PAY WITH LOOT (3.2.2):
  A Lord may spend Loot from his mat ONLY if HE is in a
  Friendly Locale, to shift Service of:
    - His own marker, OR
    - The marker of a Lord at the same Locale.
  Each Loot = 1 box right.
  IMPORTANT: A Siege Locale is never Friendly (1.3.1), so
  Besieged Lords may NEVER Pay with Loot. A Lord at a
  Stronghold Conquered FROM the enemy (and so Friendly to
  this side) MAY Pay with Loot there.
 
THE FEED-VS-PAY DISTINCTION:
  Loot can be SPENT to Pay only at Friendly Locales.
  Loot can be CONSUMED to Feed (4.8.1) anywhere — including
  at Sieges and unfriendly Locales. Provender works the same
  way (Feed only). This is why Loot is sometimes more valuable
  than Provender deep in enemy territory.
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
SHARING  (Rule 1.5.2)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Lords on the same side at the SAME Locale may SHARE Assets to
help one another:
  - Transport, Provender, Coin, and Loot are all shareable.
 
Lords NEVER share:
  - Vassals
  - Forces (units)
  - This Lord Capability cards
  - Anything else card-based.
 
CRITICAL: Sharing is USE in place, not transfer. Markers do
NOT move from one Lord's mat to another. Each Asset stays on
its owner's mat; the using Lord simply spends or relies on it
during the relevant action.
 
EXAMPLES OF SHARING:
  - A Lord shares Carts to speed a group's March (4.3.1) or to
    enable Avoid Battle when another Lord's Provender exceeds
    his own usable Transport (4.3.2-.4).
  - A Lord shares Provender or Loot to Feed Forces of a
    co-located Lord whose own supply is exhausted (4.8.1
    Sharing).
  - A Lord shares Transport during Supply along the Active
    Lord's Route (4.6).
 
WHAT SHARING DOES NOT MEAN:
  Sharing is not generosity. During Feed, Lords MUST share
  remaining Provender/Loot to feed any co-located ally short on
  supply (4.8.1). Players may not withhold.

PART II — LOCALE MECHANICS  (Section 1.3.1)
================================================================
 
LOCALE TYPES:
  Region, Town, Trade Route, Fort, Castle, Bishopric, City,
  Novgorod.
 
CONQUERABLE  (Rule 1.3.1):
  All Locales EXCEPT Regions and Towns receive Conquered
  markers when taken from the enemy. The frame border on the
  printed Locale name shows the VP value:
    Single border ........ 1 VP marker  (Forts, Castles,
                                          Trade Routes)
    Double border ........ 2 VP markers (Cities, Bishoprics)
    Triple border ........ 3 VP markers (Novgorod)
  When the original owner retakes a Locale, REMOVE all
  Conquered markers there.
 
STRONGHOLDS:
  Strongholds = Novgorod, Cities, Bishoprics, Castles, Forts.
  Strongholds can host Lords Withdrawn inside them, and can
  be Besieged and Stormed. Each Stronghold has a Siege
  Capacity (1, 2, or 3) — see Strongholds Reference.
 
  Trade Routes are Conquerable but are NOT Strongholds — no
  Capacity, no Walls, no Garrison; they flip from a Lord's
  presence without Storm.
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
FRIENDLY LOCALES  (Rule 1.3.1)
----------------------------------------------------------------
A Locale is Friendly to a side IF AND ONLY IF BOTH conditions
hold:
 
  Condition 1 — Territory or Conquered:
    The Locale is in that side's territory (Estonia or Livonia
    for Teutons; Rus for Russians), OR
    The Locale is a Stronghold Conquered by that side (i.e.,
    holds that side's Conquered markers in enemy territory).
 
  Condition 2 — Free of Enemy:
    The Locale has NO enemy Lords AT it,
    NO Unconquered Strongholds (i.e., enemy-side Strongholds
    that this side has not yet Conquered), AND
    NO Conquered markers of the OTHER color.
 
A Locale may be Friendly to NEITHER side.
 
CONSEQUENCES — A Locale is NEVER Friendly if:
  - It is a Siege Locale (4.3.5).
  - It is a Region, Town, or Trade Route in ENEMY territory,
    even if currently empty or occupied by your own Lord.
  - It has any enemy Conquered marker, even if currently
    empty of enemy Lords.
 
EXAMPLES (per the rules):
  - Vod or Volkhov, if empty: Friendly to Russians (in Rus).
  - Neva with a Conquered marker: Friendly to NEITHER side.
  - Izborsk Conquered by Teutons: Friendly to Teutons only,
    until retaken by Russians.
 
WHY FRIENDLY MATTERS:
  - Muster requires a Friendly Locale (3.4).
  - Pay with Loot requires a Friendly Locale (3.2.2).
  - Levy Transport (3.4.3) requires a Friendly Locale.
  - Levy Capabilities (3.4.4) requires a Friendly Locale.
  - Withdraw into a Stronghold requires it be Friendly to your
    side (4.3.4).
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
WAYS  (Rule 1.3.1)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Ways are the lines linking Locales. Two types exist:
 
  WATERWAYS — trace along rivers and any lakeshore.
              The Baltic Sea is NOT a Waterway (1.3.1).
              Used by: Boats (Rasputitsa/Summer),
                       Sleds (Winter only).
 
  TRACKWAYS — overland routes (portages, roads, tracks).
              Used by: Carts (Summer only),
                       Sleds (Winter only).
 
ADJACENT  (Rule 1.3.1):
  Two Locales are "adjacent" if connected by EITHER a
  Waterway OR a Trackway. Adjacency is what March, Avoid
  Battle, Retreat, Supply Routes, and Raiders Capability all
  trace along.
 
WAYS ARE SEPARATE: Trackways and Waterways are not
interchangeable for movement purposes. To go from Narwia to
Ugaunia you must first move Narwia → Uzmen or Dorpat by
Waterway, then Uzmen/Dorpat → Ugaunia by Trackway. That is
two moves.
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
SEAPORTS  (Rule 1.3.1)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Locales marked with an anchor symbol are Seaports. Seaports
matter for:
  - Sail (4.7.3): Lords can Sail between any two Seaports
    free of Unbesieged enemy Lords, in Rasputitsa or Summer.
    The Baltic Sea is not a Waterway, so the only way to
    cross it is Sail.
  - Supply (4.6.3): Ships at Seaports (Teutonic) and at
    Novgorod (Russian) can serve as Supply Sources for up to
    2 Provender each.
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
SEATS  (Rule 1.3.1)
----------------------------------------------------------------
A Lord's Seat is a Locale where his Coat of Arms is printed.
Lords Muster (Levy 3.4) only at one of their Seats. Multiple
Lords may share a Seat. Special Seat conditions:
 
  PSKOV — Yaroslav (Teutonic side) treats the Russian City of
          Pskov as a Seat ONLY while it is Conquered by the
          Teutons (black markers present).
 
  ARCHBISHOPRIC — Novgorod bears a cross icon. While the R15
                  Archbishopric Capability is in play, Novgorod
                  is an EXTRA Seat for ALL Russian Lords. For
                  Aleksandr, Andrey, and Domash (whose primary
                  Seat is already Novgorod), Novgorod under R15
                  counts as TWO Seats (per 1.3.1).
                  Aleksandr and Andrey ALSO gain Rusa as an
                  extra Seat under R15 (per their mats).
 
  COMMANDERIES — Strongholds with the Order symbol serve as
                 EXTRA Seats for the relevant Teutonic Lords
                 while the T12 Ordensburgen Capability is in
                 play.
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
"ON MAP" vs "OFF MAP"  (Rule 1.3.1)
----------------------------------------------------------------
The phrase "on the map" or "on map" means a Lord or marker is
either AT a Locale, or (for markers only) in the Veche box.
 
A Lord's CYLINDER on the Calendar is NOT "on map" — that Lord
is Ready (or set up to enter), not in play.

PART III — THE PAPAL LEGATE  (Sections 1.4.1, 3.5.1, 4.2)
================================================================
 
ENABLING:
  The Legate (purple pawn) is in play ONLY while the
  William of Modena Capability card is in effect. When the
  Teutons Levy William of Modena, place the pawn on the
  card; it enters the map at a Bishopric during the next
  Call to Arms.
 
LOCATION:
  Once on the map, the Legate sits at a Locale alongside
  Teutonic Lords. The Legate is NOT a Lord; he occupies a
  Locale but does not Battle, hold Forces, or carry Assets.
 
MOVEMENT:
  The Legate may March (4.3) or Sail (4.7.3) along with any
  Teutonic Lord he is co-located with — automatically, at the
  Lord's discretion when that Lord moves. The Legate moves
  with the Lord; he does not consume Transport.
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
LEGATE EFFECTS DURING CALL TO ARMS  (Rule 3.5.1)
----------------------------------------------------------------
During Call to Arms, the Teutonic player may take EITHER ONE
of the following:
 
  ACTION 1 — Move the Legate pawn to any Friendly Locale.
 
  ACTION 2 — USE the Legate (then return him to the William
             of Modena Capability card). Choose ONE:
 
      i.   At the Seat of a Ready Lord:
           Muster that Lord there WITHOUT rolling Fealty.
 
      ii.  At the Seat of a Lord on the Calendar:
           Slide that Lord's cylinder 1 box LEFT
           (closer to Ready).
 
      iii. At a Friendly Locale with a Lord:
           That Lord performs an immediate EXTRA Muster
           using his FULL Lordship Rating.
 
(Compare to Veche 3.5.2 — the Legate's Action iii option
mirrors the Veche's Extra-Muster option, but Legate-driven
extra Musters cost no VP.)
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
LEGATE EFFECTS DURING CAMPAIGN  (Rule 4.2 LEGATE)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Whenever a Teutonic Lord starts his Activation (his Command
card is revealed) at the SAME Locale as the Legate pawn:
  - The Teutonic player may add +1 to that Lord's Command
    Rating for the current card.
  - REMOVE the pawn back to the William of Modena Capability
    card if and when the Lord uses the extra action.
 
The Legate may grant this +1 only ONCE per appearance on the
map; using it sends him back to the card. He'll only re-enter
the map next Call to Arms (3.5.1) — and only if William of
Modena hasn't been discarded.
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
ENDANGERED — Removal Conditions  (Rules 1.4.1, 4.3.4)
----------------------------------------------------------------
If ANY of the following happen, REMOVE the Legate pawn AND
DISCARD the William of Modena Capability card:
 
  1. The Legate is with a Teutonic Lord who Avoids Battle
     (4.3.4) OR Withdraws into a Stronghold (4.3.4) OR
     Retreats (4.4.3).
 
  2. The Legate is at a Locale with one or more Russian
     Lords AND no Teutonic Lord remains there (e.g., the
     last Teutonic Lord at the Locale was just removed,
     leaving the Legate alone with Russians).
 
Once William of Modena is discarded, the Capability is gone
for the rest of the scenario. It can re-enter only if drawn
again as a Capability later — but in standard play, William
of Modena returns to the deck only when discarded down to
two This-Lord cards (which doesn't apply, because it's not
This Lord) or by 4.9.5 end-of-Campaign discard. Once
Endangered-discarded mid-Campaign, William of Modena returns
to the deck and is shuffled in for the next Levy.

PART IV — OTHER MARKERS  (Section 1.8)
================================================================
 
ROUND MARKERS:
  - Levy/Campaign marker:   tracks current phase. One per
                            Calendar; flipped between sides.
  - Victory markers:        white and black; one per side.
                            Two faces: whole number, +½.
  - Battle/Storm markers:   numbered 1–6 round markers used
                            to flag the Battle/Storm Locale
                            and to log Storm Round count.
 
SQUARE MARKERS:
  - Conquered (1VP):        5/8" square markers. White for
                            Russian-Conquered, black for
                            Teutonic-Conquered. Placed on
                            taken Strongholds and Trade
                            Routes (per VP value, 1.3.1) or
                            in the Veche box (Russian VP).
  - Walls markers:          square; placed via Russian R18
                            Stone Kremlin Capability to mark
                            a Russian Fort, City, or Novgorod
                            with Walls +1.
 
RECTANGULAR MARKERS:
  - Castle markers:         Russian R-side and Teutonic T-side.
                            Marks a Stronghold upgrade. The
                            Teuton-built Castle markers convert
                            Russian Forts via T17 Stonemasons.
                            A Russian Castle marker can be
                            replaced by a Teuton one (and vice
                            versa) on conquest.
  - Sea Trade Blocked:      placed via card events to flip off
                            the Black or Baltic Sea Trade
                            Capability for that Levy/Campaign.
 
SIEGE MARKERS:
  - Siege markers:          placed at Besieged Strongholds
                            (Siege Capacity gates how fast they
                            stack), max 4 per Locale. They
                            represent the besieging works and
                            also serve as Siegeworks (Walls) in
                            Storm or Sally (4.5.2, 4.5.3).
 
LORD-RELATED:
  - Moved/Fought markers:   placed on a Lord cylinder when the
                            Lord conducts March, Avoid Battle,
                            Battle, Siege, Storm, or Sail. Have
                            a Supply Source reminder on the
                            back (4.6.1). Removed at end of
                            each Command card after Feed/Pay/
                            Disband.
  - Numbered markers (1–6): general-purpose tokens for logging
                            actions, combat rounds, altered
                            ratings, and other temporary
                            tracking needs.
 
RAVAGED MARKERS:
  - 5/8" square markers placed on a Locale when Ravaged
    (4.7.2). White = Russian-Ravaged Teutonic Locale;
    black = Teutonic-Ravaged Russian Locale. Each is worth
    ½ VP to the side that placed it. Halved (round up) at
    end of each Rasputitsa (4.9.1 Grow).

PART V — GENERAL SETUP  (Section 2.1)
================================================================
 
LAY OUT  (Rule 2.1.1):
  - Seat players opposite each other; place the board between
    them. Allow table room on all sides for mats, cards,
    Forces, and marker pools.
  - Apply Lord cylinder STICKERS from the small sheet
    provided: white sticker on white cylinder, black on
    black, one sticker per cylinder (14 cylinders total).
  - The Battle mat (4.4.1) lives off-board until Battle/
    Storm.
 
POOLS:
  - Forces pool: all wooden unit pieces (everything except
    Lord cylinders and the Legate pawn).
  - Asset pool: Transport (Boats, Carts, Sleds, Ships),
    Provender, Coin, and Loot markers.
  - Marker pool: all other markers (1.8) — Conquered,
    Ravaged, Siege, Castle, Walls, Moved/Fought, numbered
    markers.
 
DECKS:
  Sort the playing cards into FOUR decks:
  - Russian Arts of War
  - Teutonic Arts of War
  - Russian Command (3 per Russian Lord = 18 cards)
  - Teutonic Command (3 per Teutonic Lord = 18 cards)
 
UNUSED MATS:
  Lord mats not in the current scenario sit in an Unused area
  to one side. Mats return here when a Lord Disbands but is
  not permanently removed (3.3.2).
 
OPTIONAL ITEMS:
  - Hidden Mats Option (1.5.2): one screen per player.
  - Sequence of Play foldout: one per player, kept handy.
 
SCENARIO SETUP (2.1.2):
  Choose a scenario (6.0). Agree on optional rules:
    - Hidden Mats (1.5.2)
    - Optional unit counters (1.6)
    - Advanced Vassal Service (3.4.2)
    - Bidding for Sides (6.0)
    - No Horseback Archery variant (6.0)
  Then place markers, cylinders, and Lord mats as the scenario
  prescribes (see Scenario Reference). Begin with the first
  Levy.
