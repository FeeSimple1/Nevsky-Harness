PART 1. OVERVIEW
----------------------------------------------------------------
 
Call to Arms is the FINAL step of the Levy phase, executed
AFTER both sides have completed Muster (3.4). During Call to
Arms, each side's higher religious or civil authority — the
Papal Legate (Teutonic) or the Novgorod Veche (Russian) —
may mobilize additional strength.
 
Order of execution: TEUTONIC FIRST, then RUSSIAN.
 
  3.5.1  Papal Legate            (Teutons)
  3.5.2  Novgorod Veche          (Russians)
  3.5.3  Discard "This Levy"     (both sides)
 
A side may always elect to do nothing in its segment.
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
PART 2. SEQUENCE OF OPERATIONS (atomic order)
----------------------------------------------------------------
 
STEP 1. Teutonic 3.5.1 Papal Legate.
  1a. CHECK: Is William of Modena Capability in effect?
        NO  -> Teutons skip Call to Arms. Go to Step 2.
        YES -> continue.
  1b. ARRIVES: If Legate pawn is on the William of Modena card
       at the start of Call to Arms, Teutons MAY place the pawn
       at any Bishopric (Riga, Dorpat, Leal, or Reval).
  1c. ACTIVE: Once the Legate pawn is on the map, Teutons MAY
       choose AT MOST ONE of the following:
        (a) Move the pawn to any Friendly Locale (1.3.1), OR
        (b) USE the Legate (then return pawn to William of
            Modena card) to do exactly ONE of:
              i.  At the Seat of a Ready Lord:
                  Muster that Lord there (3.4.1) WITHOUT
                  rolling Fealty.
              ii. At the Seat of a Lord on the Calendar:
                  Slide that Lord's cylinder 1 box LEFT.
              iii.At a Friendly Locale with a Lord:
                  That Lord performs an immediate EXTRA Muster
                  using his FULL Lordship Rating (3.4).
 
STEP 2. Russian 3.5.2 Novgorod Veche.
  2a. SEA-TRADE COIN (if Capabilities in effect):
        If R8 Black Sea Trade in effect AND not blocked:
          add 1 Coin to the Veche box.
        If R9 Baltic Sea Trade in effect AND not blocked AND
        season is not Early/Late Winter:
          add 2 Coin to the Veche box.
        These Coin additions can occur at any time during
        Russian Call to Arms, including in the same Levy in
        which the Capability was just Mustered.
        Cap: Veche box holds at most 8 Coin (excess lost).
  2b. VECHE ACTION: Russians may either DO NOTHING or take
        EXACTLY ONE of the following:
        Option A  SLIDE LEFT (cost 1 VP):
          Remove one 1VP Conquered marker from Veche box
          (and slide the white Victory marker on the
          Calendar one box left to match) to slide one Lord's
          cylinder on the Calendar 2 BOXES LEFT.
        Option B  AUTO-MUSTER (cost 1 VP):
          Remove one 1VP Conquered marker from the Veche box
          to automatically Levy one Ready Russian Lord (3.4.1)
          WITHOUT rolling Fealty.
          NOTE: Aleksandr can ONLY enter play via this option
          (no Lord may Muster him).
        Option C  EXTRA MUSTER (cost 1 VP):
          Remove one 1VP Conquered marker from the Veche box
          to enable one Russian Lord at a Friendly Locale
          (never at a Siege) to perform an immediate EXTRA
          Muster using his Lordship Rating (3.4).
        Option D  DECLINE (gains 1 VP):
          Slide Aleksandr and/or Andrey, whichever is Ready
          (cylinder at or left of the current Levy box), to
          1 box RIGHT of the current Levy.
          If BOTH are Ready, BOTH cylinders MUST be slid.
          Add ONE white 1VP Conquered marker to the Veche
          box (single marker, even if both princes were slid).
          Slide the white Victory marker on the Calendar one
          box right to match.
          Cap: Veche box holds at most 8 VP markers (excess
          lost).
 
STEP 3. 3.5.3 Discard Events.
  Both sides discard any Events that applied only to "This
  Levy" (3.1.3). Levy ends. Flip the Levy/Campaign turn
  marker to Campaign (2.2.2, 4.0).

PART 3. TEUTONIC CALL TO ARMS — DETAILS (3.5.1)
----------------------------------------------------------------
 
PRECONDITION (1.4.1, 3.5.1 INDISPOSED):
  The Legate is in play if and only if the William of Modena
  Capability (T13) is currently in effect on the Teutonic
  side (1.9.1, 3.4.4).
  Without William of Modena, Teutons SKIP Call to Arms
  entirely — no placement, no movement, no use.
 
LEGATE PAWN LOCATIONS:
  Either: on the William of Modena card (off-map), OR
  At a map Locale (entered via "ARRIVES" or moved with a
  Lord during Campaign, 1.4.1, 4.2).
 
THE FOUR BISHOPRICS (placement targets for ARRIVES):
  Riga, Dorpat, Leal, Reval.
  These are the Teutonic religious centers. (The Russian
  Archbishopric at Novgorod is NOT a Teutonic Bishopric.)
 
LEGATE OPTIONS DURING CALL TO ARMS (choose at most ONE):
  Option 1: Move the pawn to any Friendly Locale (1.3.1).
            The pawn stays on the map; no Lord effect.
  Option 2: USE the Legate, then return the pawn to the
            William of Modena card. The pawn must currently
            be at a Locale that satisfies one of the three
            sub-options below. The chosen sub-option
            determines what the use does:
 
  Sub-option 2a (LEGATE AT SEAT OF READY LORD):
    Auto-Muster that Lord at that Seat per 3.4.1 procedure
    (place cylinder, mat, starting Forces/Assets/Vassals,
    Service marker = Service Rating boxes right of Levy).
    NO Fealty roll required.
 
  Sub-option 2b (LEGATE AT SEAT OF LORD ON CALENDAR):
    Slide that Lord's cylinder 1 box LEFT on the Calendar.
    This may make a not-Ready Lord become Ready.
 
  Sub-option 2c (LEGATE AT FRIENDLY LOCALE WITH ANY LORD):
    That Lord (must be Mustered and at this Friendly Locale)
    performs an immediate EXTRA Muster using his FULL
    Lordship Rating.  All Muster options (3.4.1–3.4.4) are
    available to him — Levy other Lords, Levy Vassals, Levy
    Transport, Levy Capabilities — at this moment, in
    addition to whatever he did during the regular Muster
    segment.
 
ENDANGERED (1.4.1):
  This is a Campaign-phase risk, not a Call to Arms event,
  but it removes the Legate pawn and discards William of
  Modena, which then prevents Call to Arms next Levy. Watch
  for it.
  Trigger conditions: the Legate is with a Teutonic Lord who
  Avoids Battle, Withdraws, or Retreats (4.3.4, 4.4.3), OR is
  in a Locale with any Russian Lord(s) and no Teutonic Lord.
  Effect: remove the pawn from the map; discard William of
  Modena.

PART 4. RUSSIAN CALL TO ARMS — DETAILS (3.5.2)
----------------------------------------------------------------
 
WHAT THE VECHE HOLDS (1.3.3, 1.4.2):
  Two distinct token types in the Veche box:
    Conquered (1VP) markers — white, used for Veche
      actions A/B/C and produced by Veche action D
      (also seeded by scenario setup).
    Coin markers — gold, used during Pay (3.2.1) to shift
      any non-Besieged Russian Lord's Service. NOT spent
      during Call to Arms itself.
  Caps: max 8 VP markers AND max 8 Coin in the Veche box;
        any excess is lost (1.4.2 WASTAGE).
 
CALL TO ARMS PROCEEDS EVEN IF NOVGOROD IS CONQUERED.
  The Veche may continue its Call to Arms even while the
  Novgorod Locale is held by the Teutons (3.5, 1.4.2).
  However:
    If Teutons CONQUER Novgorod (4.5), all Coin in the
      Veche box is removed. (If Sacked, that Coin is added
      to Spoils, 4.5.2.)
    VP markers in the Veche box are NOT removed by
      Conquest of Novgorod.
    R8 Black Sea Trade: blocked while Novgorod OR Lovat is
      Conquered by Teutons; resumes if Russians retake.
    R9 Baltic Sea Trade: blocked while Novgorod OR Neva is
      Conquered, OR while Teutons have more Ships than Rus;
      season-restricted to non-Winter regardless.
 
VECHE ACTION OPTIONS — DETAILS:
 
  Option A. SLIDE LEFT (slide one Lord 2 boxes left).
    Cost: remove 1× white 1VP Conquered marker from Veche
          box. Slide the white Victory marker on the
          Calendar 1 box left.
    Target: ANY ONE Lord cylinder, on the Calendar OR on
            map. (Practical use: princes still ahead on the
            Calendar.)
    Effect: 2 boxes LEFT (per Second Edition; PAC says
            1 box and is OBSOLETE).
    Note: A Lord whose cylinder is on the map cannot be
          slid by this option (no Calendar position to move).
          The Russian source files explicitly call this a
          slide of a "Lord cylinder on the Calendar."
    Use case: bring a not-yet-Ready Lord into Ready range
              (cylinder ends up at or left of Levy marker,
              i.e. eligible for Muster).
 
  Option B. AUTO-MUSTER (Levy a Ready Lord without rolling).
    Cost: remove 1× 1VP Conquered marker from Veche box.
          Adjust white Victory.
    Target: any READY Russian Lord (cylinder at or left of
            current Levy box, with at least one free Seat).
    Effect: place that Lord per 3.4.1 procedure WITHOUT a
            Fealty roll (cylinder at chosen free Seat, mat
            in front of player, starting Forces/Assets/
            available Vassal Service markers, Service
            marker = Service Rating boxes right of Levy).
    SPECIAL RULE: Aleksandr has NO Fealty rating and may
            NEVER be Mustered by any Lord (3.4.1). The
            Veche auto-Muster (Option B) is the ONLY way
            Aleksandr can enter play.
 
  Option C. EXTRA MUSTER (a Russian Lord uses his Lordship).
    Cost: remove 1× 1VP Conquered marker from Veche box.
          Adjust white Victory.
    Target: ONE already-Mustered Russian Lord at a Friendly
            Locale (1.3.1). Never a Lord at a Siege.
    Effect: that Lord performs an immediate EXTRA Muster
            using his FULL Lordship Rating (3.4). All
            Muster options 3.4.1–3.4.4 are available
            (Levy other Lords, Vassals, Transport,
            Capabilities), in addition to whatever he did
            during the regular Muster segment.
    Note: A Lord who was JUST brought onto the map this
          Call to Arms (e.g., via Option B) CANNOT be the
          recipient of Option C in the same Call to Arms,
          per the general Muster rule that newly arrived
          Lords cannot use their Lordship in the same
          Muster segment (3.4). The Rules text explicitly
          extends this: "He may be able to do so during
          that Levy's Call to Arms (3.5)" — meaning a Lord
          Mustered DURING the standard Muster segment can
          use his Lordship via Option C, but a Lord
          Mustered VIA Option B (auto-Muster) within Call
          to Arms cannot then turn around and be the
          subject of Option C in the same Call to Arms.
 
  Option D. DECLINE (gain 1 VP for the Veche).
    Cost: NONE (this is the income action).
    Target: Aleksandr and/or Andrey, whichever has a
            cylinder that is READY (at or left of current
            Levy box).
    Procedure:
      1. Identify which of Aleksandr / Andrey are Ready.
      2. If exactly one is Ready, slide that one to 1 box
         RIGHT of current Levy.
      3. If BOTH are Ready, BOTH cylinders MUST be slid to
         1 box right of current Levy. (You may not decline
         only one when both are Ready.)
      4. Add EXACTLY ONE white 1VP Conquered marker to the
         Veche box (one marker total, even when both were
         slid). Slide the white Victory marker on the
         Calendar 1 box right to match.
    Cap: Veche VP cap is 8. If the box already holds 8 VP,
         the new marker is forfeit (1.4.2 WASTAGE).
    Worked example (per Rules of Play): It is Call to Arms
    of 40-Days box 7; Andrey and Aleksandr cylinders are in
    box 5. The Russian player declines both, sliding their
    cylinders to box 8 and adding a 1VP Conquered marker to
    the Veche box.
    Reminder: only the Veche may Levy Aleksandr (3.4.1).

PART 5. SEA-TRADE COIN ADDITIONS (R8, R9)
----------------------------------------------------------------
 
R8 BLACK SEA TRADE.
  Card text: "Each Call to Arms, add 1 Coin to Veche unless
  Novgorod or Lovat Conquered."
  Tip: Add the Coin once at any time during Russian Call to
  Arms, including in the same Levy as Mustering the
  Capability.
  Marker handling: place the appropriate Coin marker on the
  card as a reminder, then move it to the Veche box during
  Russian Call to Arms.
  Blocked: while Novgorod OR Lovat is Conquered by Teutons.
  Place a "SEA TRADE Blocked" marker on the card while
  blocked. If Russians retake the Conquered Locale, the
  benefit resumes the next Call to Arms.
 
R9 BALTIC SEA TRADE.
  Card text: "Each non-Winter Call to Arms, add 2 Coin to
  Veche unless Teutons have more Ships than Rus OR Novgorod
  or Neva Conquered."
  Tip: Same handling as R8.
  Blocked: while Novgorod OR Neva is Conquered, OR while
  Teutons have more Ships than Rus, OR during Early Winter
  or Late Winter (no Coin in those seasons regardless).
  Ship count: R16 Lodya may double a Russian Lord's Boats
  for purposes of this comparison; T18 Cogs may also count
  toward the Teutonic Ship total. Compare totals at the
  moment Coin is to be added.
 
WHERE THE COIN GOES:
  Directly to the Veche box (not to any Lord's mat).
  These Coin tokens are spent later, during Pay (3.2.1), to
  shift the Service of any non-Besieged Russian Lord by
  1 box right per Coin spent. Veche Coin can travel to any
  Russian Lord on the map regardless of Locale (the only
  cross-locale Coin in the game).
 
WHEN COIN IS ADDED RELATIVE TO THE VECHE ACTION:
  The Russian player may add the Coin BEFORE or AFTER
  taking the Veche action (Options A–D) within the Russian
  Call to Arms segment. The "once any time during Russian
  Call to Arms" wording is permissive.
 
VP MARKERS — NOT FROM SEA TRADE:
  Sea-Trade Capabilities add Coin only, never VP. The only
  routes to put VP markers into the Veche are scenario
  setup and Option D (Decline) above.
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
PART 6. DISCARD EVENTS — 3.5.3
----------------------------------------------------------------
 
After both Call to Arms segments, both sides discard any
Events that applied only to "This Levy" (3.1.3). These were
revealed during the Arts of War step at the start of the
current Levy and remained in effect for the entire Levy.
 
Hold Events do NOT discard here. They remain hidden in the
holding player's area until played per their text or
discarded later (e.g., during End Campaign Reset, 4.9.5).
 
"This Campaign" Events also do NOT discard here; they
discard at end of Campaign (4.9.5).

PART 7. VECHE BOX MECHANICS — CONSOLIDATED
----------------------------------------------------------------
 
CONTENTS OF VECHE BOX:
  - White 1VP Conquered markers (max 8).
  - Gold Coin markers (max 8).
 
ADDING VP MARKERS:
  - Scenario setup (e.g., Pleskau, Watland, Peipus all start
    with at least one VP marker; Peipus starts with 4).
  - Veche Option D (Decline) during Russian Call to Arms.
    There are no other in-game routes to add VP to the box.
 
REMOVING VP MARKERS:
  - Spending one for Veche Options A, B, or C during
    Russian Call to Arms (3.5.2).
  - Each spent VP slides the white Victory marker on the
    Calendar 1 box LEFT to reflect the loss of Russian VP.
 
ADDING COIN:
  - R8 Black Sea Trade during Russian Call to Arms.
  - R9 Baltic Sea Trade during non-Winter Russian Call to
    Arms (subject to blocks).
  - There is no other in-game route to add Coin to the box.
 
REMOVING COIN:
  - Pay step (3.2.1): spend Veche Coin to shift the Service
    of any non-Besieged Russian Lord 1 box right per Coin.
  - Conquest of Novgorod by Teutons removes ALL Coin from
    the Veche box (1.4.2). If Novgorod was Sacked, that
    Coin is added to Teutonic Spoils (4.5.2). VP markers
    are not affected by Conquest.
 
VICTORY POINT BOOKKEEPING:
  Each VP marker in the Veche box corresponds to one box of
  white Victory marker advance on the Calendar (toward the
  Russian end). Adding a VP marker to the box advances the
  Victory marker right by 1; removing one slides it left by
  1. Adjust Calendar Victory whenever the box VP count
  changes.

PART 8. INTERACTIONS WITH EVENTS AND CAPABILITIES
----------------------------------------------------------------
 
EVENTS THAT REMOVE THE LEGATE / DISCARD WILLIAM OF MODENA:
  R15 Death of the Pope.
    Drawn during Arts of War (3.1.3). If William of Modena
    is currently in play as a Capability, immediately
    discard it and remove the Legate pawn from the map
    (even if Besieged). For the rest of THIS LEVY, no
    William of Modena may be Levied.
    Effect on Call to Arms: Teutons SKIP Call to Arms this
    Levy (no Legate, no William of Modena Capability).
    The card discards at end of this Levy. William of
    Modena may be re-Levied in a later 40 Days.
    Death of the Pope can occur multiple times in a game.
 
EVENTS THAT BLOCK A LORD'S LORDSHIP DURING CALL TO ARMS:
  R11 Valdemar.
    "On Calendar, shift Knud & Abel OR their Service up to
    1 box; this Levy, no Muster of or by them."
    Tip: blocks Knud & Abel's use of Lordship, INCLUDING
    during Call to Arms.
    Effect: Knud & Abel cannot be the recipient of the
    Legate's Sub-option 2c (Extra Muster) or Auto-Muster
    sub-option 2a this Levy.
  R17 Dietrich von Grüningen leads Order to Kurland.
    "On Calendar, shift Andreas OR Rudolf OR their Service
    1 box; this Levy, no Muster of or by them."
    Tip: blocks Andreas's and Rudolf's use of Lordship,
    INCLUDING during Call to Arms and via any Teutonic
    Events. Andreas and Rudolf cannot be the recipient of
    Legate Sub-option 2c, nor can the Legate auto-Muster
    them via 2a, this Levy.
 
EVENTS THAT SHIFT ALEKSANDR / ANDREY ON THE CALENDAR
(affecting whether Decline is even available):
  T1  Grand Prince favors a son.
    On Calendar, shift Aleksandr OR Andrey OR furthest-right
    Service of either by 2 boxes (Teutons choose direction).
    Discard immediately. (Removed from deck while William
    of Modena Capability is in play.)
  T12 Khan Baty — Mongols' return feared.
    On Calendar, shift Aleksandr OR Andrey OR Service of
    either up to 2 boxes (Teutons choose direction).
    Discard immediately.
  R10 Batu Khan — Mongols turn west.
    On Calendar, shift Andreas cylinder OR Service up to 2
    boxes (Russians choose direction). Discard immediately.
 
HOLD EVENTS THAT GRANT LORDSHIP +2 IN CALL TO ARMS:
  These all read "shift cylinder 2 Calendar boxes OR
  Lordship +2." The Lordship +2 can be used during the
  Muster segment OR during the Call to Arms segment of the
  same Levy. Each is one-shot ("Discard the moment used").
    R8  Prince of Polotsk — any Russian Lord.
    R13 Pelgui — Vladislav OR Karelians.
    T7  Tverdilo — Hermann OR Yaroslav.
    T8  Teutonic Fervor — Rudolf only.
    T17 Dietrich von Grüningen focuses Order on Rus —
        Andreas OR Rudolf.
  Practical use in Call to Arms: a Lord receiving Legate
  Sub-option 2c or Veche Option C (extra Muster at full
  Lordship) can stack his Lordship +2 to perform more
  Muster actions in that extra Muster.
  Caveat (R8 Prince of Polotsk, T8 Teutonic Fervor, T17):
  the receiving Lord may use the bonus to Levy the
  Capability on the same card as part of that extra Muster.
  R13 Pelgui: only Vladislav (not Karelians) may use the
  bonus to Levy the Capability on the same card.
 
CAPABILITIES MUSTERED MID-LEVY THAT TAKE EFFECT IN THIS
SAME CALL TO ARMS:
  R8 Black Sea Trade: explicitly may add its Coin "in the
  same Levy as Mustering the Capability."
  R9 Baltic Sea Trade: same per R8 reference.
  William of Modena (T13): if Mustered during this Levy's
  Muster segment, the Legate pawn enters play, and the
  Teutons may use it normally during the immediately
  following Call to Arms (place at any Bishopric per
  ARRIVES, then optionally USE).

PART 9. GLOSSARY OF KEY TERMS
----------------------------------------------------------------
 
READY (3.4.1, 2.2):
  A Lord whose cylinder is in a 40-Days box AT or LEFT of
  the current Levy marker. (Cylinders RIGHT of the Levy
  marker are not Ready.)
 
FREE SEAT (3.4.1):
  A Seat free of enemy Lords and not Conquered by the
  enemy. Required for any Muster (whether by Lord, Veche,
  or Legate).
 
FRIENDLY LOCALE (1.3.1):
  All four conditions met:
    1. Locale is in own side's territory (Estonia/Livonia
       for Teutons, Rus for Russians) OR is a Stronghold
       Conquered by the own side.
    2. No enemy Lord at the Locale.
    3. No enemy Stronghold at the Locale.
    4. No enemy Conquered marker at the Locale.
  A Siege Locale (4.3.5) is NEVER Friendly to either side.
 
SEAT (1.3.1):
  A Locale bearing a Lord's Coat of Arms. Extra Seats may
  be granted by Capabilities:
    R15 Archbishopric — Novgorod is an extra Seat for ALL
        Russian Lords (so Novgorod = 2 Seats for Aleksandr,
        Andrey, Domash; for some, e.g. Aleksandr, Andrey
        per their mats, Rusa is also an Extra Seat under
        the cross icon).
    T12 Ordensburgen — each Teutonic Commandery is an
        extra Seat for the indicated Teutonic Lords.
    Pskov — a Seat for Yaroslav while Conquered by
        Teutons.
 
BISHOPRIC (1.3.1, 1.4.1):
  Teutonic religious Stronghold. The four are Riga, Dorpat,
  Leal, Reval. The Legate's ARRIVES placement during Call
  to Arms must be at one of these four (no need for it to
  be Friendly per 3.5.1, though by default they will be on
  the Teutonic side).
 
BESIEGED (4.3.5):
  A Lord at a Locale where the enemy has begun a Siege.
  Besieged Lords cannot Muster, cannot use Veche Coin to
  pay them via 3.2.1 (only their own or another Besieged
  Lord's Coin works), and cannot be the recipient of Veche
  Option C.
 
LORDSHIP RATING (1.5.3):
  The number of Levy actions the Lord can take during a
  Muster segment. Used in Call to Arms only via Legate
  Sub-option 2c (Extra Muster) and Veche Option C (Extra
  Muster), both of which use the recipient Lord's FULL
  Lordship Rating, plus any Hold-Event bonus active at
  that moment.

PART 10. DECISION CHECKLIST FOR SELF-PLAY
----------------------------------------------------------------
 
TEUTONIC PRE-CHECKS (3.5.1):
  [ ] Is William of Modena currently in play?
        If NO  -> skip to Russian segment.
        If YES -> continue.
  [ ] Where is the Legate pawn?
        On William of Modena card -> ARRIVES allowed: place
            at any Bishopric.
        On the map at a Locale -> evaluate USE options.
  [ ] If pawn is on the map, evaluate which Locale Class
      the pawn currently occupies:
        Seat of a Ready Lord                   -> 2a available
        Seat of a Lord on the Calendar         -> 2b available
        Friendly Locale with any Mustered Lord -> 2c available
        None of the above                      -> only Option 1
                                                  (move pawn)
  [ ] Score each available USE option against scenario goals:
        2a worth most when target Lord has low Fealty
            (avoiding the failed-roll cost).
        2b worth most when target Lord is 1 box off Ready.
        2c worth most when target Lord has high Lordship and
            sits on multiple useful Levy targets, especially
            with a Hold Event giving Lordship +2.
  [ ] If using USE: pawn returns to William of Modena card.
      If using Option 1 (move): pawn stays on map.
 
RUSSIAN PRE-CHECKS (3.5.2):
  [ ] Is Novgorod Conquered by Teutons?
        If YES: Veche may still act, but Coin (if any) was
        wiped at Conquest. R8 still works unless Lovat is
        also Conquered. R9 also requires Neva not Conquered.
  [ ] If R8 in effect and not blocked, add 1 Coin to Veche.
  [ ] If R9 in effect and not blocked AND season is non-
      Winter, add 2 Coin to Veche.
  [ ] How many VP markers are in the Veche box?
        0      -> Options A, B, C unavailable; only D or
                  do nothing.
        1–7    -> All four options available; pick best.
        8      -> Decline cap reached: Option D produces no
                  VP gain (still slides princes if you choose
                  it, but the marker is forfeit).
  [ ] Are Aleksandr or Andrey Ready?
        If neither -> Option D unavailable.
        If exactly one -> Option D slides only him.
        If both -> Option D MUST slide both.
  [ ] Score each option against scenario tempo:
        A worth most when sliding either prince (or another
            Lord) by 2 boxes brings him to Ready in time
            for next Levy or this segment.
        B worth most for getting Aleksandr in (his only
            entry route) or for low-Fealty princes.
        C worth most when a high-Lordship Lord (Aleksandr 4,
            Andrey 3) sits on multiple Levy targets,
            especially with a Hold Event.
        D worth most early game when the front is quiet and
            VP banking yields more flexibility than a prince
            on the map. Note Veche cap is 8.
 
GENERAL TIPS (from project sources):
  - Decline early, summon late. Each declined VP becomes
    flexible Veche resources later.
  - Veche Coin paid via 3.2.1 reaches any non-Besieged
    Russian Lord, anywhere — the Russians' single biggest
    logistical lever.
  - Hoarded Veche Coin caps at 8; spend proactively to
    avoid wastage (1.4.2 WASTAGE).
  - Aleksandr in Pleskau scenario: cylinder is removed
    from play (he is not in the deck for that scenario).
    Verify scenario rules before assuming Aleksandr is
    available.

PART 11. COMMON ERRORS AND OBSOLETE RULES
----------------------------------------------------------------
 
OBSOLETE 1ST EDITION TEXT (do NOT use):
  PAC text says Veche slides a cylinder "1 Calendar box
  left." This is OBSOLETE. Second Edition is 2 BOXES LEFT.
 
OBSOLETE PAC EXCEPTION (deleted in 2nd Edition):
  PAC text says "(not Aleksandr, Andrey only by Aleksandr)."
  The Andrey-only-by-Aleksandr restriction was REMOVED in
  Second Edition. In 2E, only Aleksandr is restricted to
  Veche-only Muster; Andrey may be Mustered like any other
  Russian Lord.
 
COMMON ERRORS:
  - Adding Sea-Trade Coin to a Lord's mat instead of the
    Veche box. Sea-Trade Coin always goes to the Veche.
  - Spending Veche Coin during Call to Arms. Coin is spent
    during PAY (3.2.1), not Call to Arms. Call to Arms
    spends VP markers, not Coin.
  - Sliding only one prince when both are Ready under
    Option D. The rule REQUIRES both be slid if both are
    Ready.
  - Adding 2 VP markers when both princes are declined.
    The decline yields ONE marker total, regardless of
    whether one or both princes were slid.
  - Forgetting to slide the white Calendar Victory marker
    when VP markers enter or leave the Veche box.
  - Trying to use Veche Option C on a Lord who is at a
    Siege. Option C requires a Friendly Locale; Sieges
    are never Friendly (1.3.1).
  - Trying to use Legate Sub-option 2a or 2c on Knud & Abel
    while R11 Valdemar is in effect this Levy, or on
    Andreas/Rudolf while R17 Dietrich is in effect this
    Levy. Both Events block use of those Lords' Lordship
    AND block their Muster.
  - Skipping Russian Call to Arms because Novgorod is
    Conquered. Russians may still act with the Veche.
    Only Coin is lost to Conquest; VP markers and Veche
    actions persist.
  - Forgetting that Aleksandr's only entry path is Veche
    Option B. The Legate cannot Muster Aleksandr (no
    Fealty rating to roll against), and no Lord can
    Muster him.
  - Using the Legate to grant a Command bonus during Call
    to Arms. The Legate's Command bonus is a CAMPAIGN-only
    use (4.2). Call to Arms uses are limited to the four
    listed in 3.5.1.
