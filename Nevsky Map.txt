NEVSKY MAP REFERENCE
====================
 
LOCALE TYPES (per Rule 1.3.1):
Regions, Towns, Trade Routes, Forts, Castles, Bishoprics, Cities, Novgorod.
Strongholds = Forts, Castles, Bishoprics, Cities, Novgorod (can be Besieged/Stormed).
Conquerable = all except Regions and Towns (receive VP markers when taken).
VP marker count: 1 for Forts/Castles, 2 for Bishoprics/Cities, 3 for Novgorod.

LORD SEATS
===============================================================
Each Lord Musters at one of his Seats (per Rule 3.4.1). A Seat
must be free of enemy Lords AND not Conquered by the enemy to
serve as a Muster Locale.

TEUTONIC LORDS
--------------
  Andreas von Felben       - Riga, Wenden
  Hermann                  - Dorpat, Odenpah
  Heinrich                 - Leal
  Knud & Abel              - Reval, Wesenberg
  Rudolf von Kassel        - Wenden
  Yaroslav (Exile of Pskov)- Odenpah
                            + Pskov (only while Conquered by the
                              Teutons; black Conquered markers
                              required per Rule 3.4.1)

  Conditional default for ALL Teutonic Lords:
    + Commanderies (any Stronghold marked with the Order symbol)
      become Extra Seats while T12 Ordensburgen Capability is
      in play. A Teutonic Lord starting his Command card already
      at a Commandery Locale gains Command +1 on that card.
      Wenden is the only Commandery explicitly named in the
      rules text (the example "Wenden would be two Seats each
      for Andreas and Rudolf"); Fellin is confirmed as a
      Commandery via the Playbook example of Heinrich Mustering
      there. Other Commanderies are identified by the Order
      symbol on the map.

===============================================================
NOTES AND OBSERVATIONS
===============================================================
 
LAKES (terrain only, not Locales): Peipus, Pskov, Ilmen, Ladoga.
Waterways trace along their shores; the lakes themselves
do not host cylinders or markers.
 
WINTER HIGHWAYS: Sleds are the only Waterway transport in
Winter, but they move freely. The Lake Peipus long waterway
becomes a 4-way junction (Dorpat / Narwia / Gdov / Uzmen all
mutually adjacent). Rasputitsa shuts down all Waterway and
Trackway movement options that don't use Sleds or Boats; the
spring thaw is the most restrictive season. The Battle on the
Ice (April 5, 1242) sits at the late Winter / Rasputitsa hinge.
 
ASYMMETRIC WATER NETWORKS: Russians have a year-round river
spine (Volkhov - Ladoga - Novgorod - Rusa - Lovat). Teutons
have only the Fellin-Dorpat-Odenpah triangle plus the Adsel-
Wenden-Riga thread. Crossing Lake Peipus outside Winter
requires Sail or detour through Ugaunia.
 
BISHOPRIC IMBALANCE: Four Teutonic Bishoprics (Riga, Dorpat,
Leal, Reval) versus one Russian Archbishopric at Novgorod.
Each Bishopric is worth 2 VP markers when conquered; Novgorod
is worth 3. With William of Modena in play, the Legate pawn
can be placed at any Bishopric for a free Call to Arms Levy.
 
LOCALES WITH NO TRACKWAY (Waterway-only):
  Riga, Luga, Volkhov, Lovat, Rusa.
 
LOCALES WITH ONLY ONE TRACKWAY:
  Pernau, Fellin (Livonia)
  Karelia, Ladoga, Shelon River, Zheltsy, Gdov, Uzmen,
  Ostrov, Sorot River, Velikaya River, Velikiye Luki (Rus)
