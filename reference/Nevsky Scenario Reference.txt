NEVSKY SCENARIOS — Setup Reference
==================================
Compiled per Rules of Play (Second Edition, 2023) Section 6.0 and the
Second Edition Changes Summary. Where the two conflict, Second Edition
Rules of Play govern. Historical flavor text from the rulebook is
omitted; only setup, span, and gameplay-relevant rules are captured.
 
The Quickstart Scenario noted in the Second Edition Changes Summary
("Add Quickstart Scenario setup, Battle mini-game") is published in
the Second Edition Background Book, which is NOT among the project
files. Quickstart setup is therefore not reproduced here.
 
 
================================================================
COMMON SETUP PROCEDURE  (Rule 6.0)
================================================================
Before choosing a scenario, agree on which optional rules are in
use:
  - Hidden Mats option (1.5.2)
  - Optional unit counters (1.6) — substitute counters for wood
  - Advanced Vassal Service rule (3.4.2)
  - Bidding for sides (added 2nd Ed; see below)
  - No Horseback Archery variant (see below)
 
Set up table layout per 2.1, then perform scenario setup:
 
MAP AND CALENDAR:
  Place markers and cylinders on the map and Calendar exactly as
  the scenario lists.
 
LORD MATS:
  Set out each Mustered Lord's mat as if just Levied (3.4.1):
    - Place that Lord's starting Forces on the mat.
    - Place that Lord's starting Assets on the mat.
    - Place all available Vassals' Service markers (Coat of Arms
      up) onto the mat — Vassal Forces are NOT placed; they enter
      only as the Lord later Musters them (3.4.2).
    - Special Vassals (Summer Crusaders, Steppe Warriors,
      Mongols/Kipchaqs) are NOT placed at setup; they require
      their respective Capabilities (T11 Crusade, R10 Steppe
      Warriors).
 
TRANSPORT CHOICE AT SETUP:
  For mats that show "any" Transport, the player (Teutonic, then
  Russian) selects any single Transport type INCLUDING Ships.
  For mats that show "no Ship", the player selects any single
  Transport type EXCEPT Ships. Some Lords begin with a fixed
  number of Ships already specified.
 
FIRST LEVY:
  Begin play with the first Levy. Each side shuffles its Arts of
  War deck and randomly draws two cards as Capabilities (3.1.1
  through 3.1.2).
 
 
================================================================
BIDDING FOR SIDES  (added 2nd Ed; Rule 6.0)
================================================================
After choosing a scenario but BEFORE assigning sides, players may
bid to play the Russians:
 
  1. Each player secretly puts zero, one, or more dice under a
     cupped hand.
  2. Reveal simultaneously. Total pips showing is that player's
     bid.
  3. The player with the LOWER bid takes the Russians, and adds
     1VP markers equal to that bid to the Veche box (adjusts the
     Russian victory total accordingly).
  4. If bids tied, add that number of 1VP markers to the Veche
     and assign sides randomly.
 
 
================================================================
NO HORSEBACK ARCHERY VARIANT  (Rule 6.0)
================================================================
Optional. If used:
  - Asiatic Horse units' Defense rolls always succeed only on
    "1" (treated as Unarmored), instead of using the Evade
    Defense rule.
  - All other Asiatic Horse properties are unchanged.
 
 
================================================================
UNIVERSAL VICTORY RULES  (Rule 5.0)
================================================================
A side may win immediately during play; otherwise victory is
determined at scenario end.
 
VICTORY POINTS (Rule 5.1):
  - 1 VP per Conquered marker of your color on the map (including
    those held in the Veche box).
  - 1 VP per Castle marker of your color on the map.
  - ½ VP per Ravaged marker of your color on the map.
  - PLESKAU SCENARIO ONLY: +1 VP per enemy Lord removed from the
    map by any means.
 
Track VP on the Calendar's numbered 40-Days boxes (using the
round Victory markers, on the +½ side as appropriate). Cap is
17½ VP — any excess earned is forfeit.
 
CAMPAIGN VICTORY (Rule 5.2):
  If at any moment during a Campaign one side has zero Mustered
  Lords on the map, the game ends immediately and the OTHER side
  wins, regardless of VP.
 
END-OF-SCENARIO VICTORY (Rule 5.3):
  At end of the scenario's final Campaign, the side with higher
  VP wins. Ties are draws — EXCEPT in Watland, where ties are
  Russian wins (per the Watland-specific victory clause below).

SCENARIO 1 — PLESKAU (1240)
================================================================
Span:         2 turns (one Season).
              Begin first Levy of Summer 1240 (40-Days box 1).
              Play through end of Summer 1240 (box 2).
              Teutons on the offensive.
 
Markers on Map:
  None.
 
Mustered Lords:
  Teutons:
    Hermann (Marshal) at Dorpat
    Knud & Abel       at Reval
    Yaroslav          at Odenpäh
  Russians:
    Gavrilo  at Pskov
    Vladislav at Neva
 
Veche:
  One white 1VP Conquered marker.
 
Calendar:
  Box 1: white Victory marker, Levy marker, Rudolf cylinder,
         Domash cylinder.
  Box 2: Yaroslav Service marker.
  Box 3: Knud & Abel Service marker, Vladislav Service marker.
  Box 4: Hermann Service marker, Gavrilo Service marker.
 
Remove from Play:
  All cylinders, mats, and Service markers of every Lord NOT
  listed above (i.e., remove Andreas, Heinrich, Aleksandr, Andrey,
  Karelians).
 
Special Rules:
  - Per 2nd Edition: BEFORE play begins, each side removes its
    three "No Event" Arts of War cards from its deck.
  - The 2nd Ed Changes Summary lists this as the most significant
    Pleskau update; the 1st Ed Pleskau kept the No Events in.
 
Victory:
  In addition to the universal VP sources (5.1), in this scenario
  ONLY: earn 1 VP each time an enemy Lord Disbands or is removed
  from the map. Track on the Calendar with the round markers
  provided.

SCENARIO 2 — WATLAND (1241)
================================================================
Span:         Up to 5 turns.
              Begin second Levy of Early Winter 1240 (40-Days
              box 4). Play through end of Rasputitsa 1241 (box 8).
              Teutons on the offensive.
 
Markers on Map:
  - Black Conquered marker at Izborsk.
  - Two black Conquered markers at Pskov.
  - Black Ravaged markers at Pskov and Dubrovno.
 
Mustered Lords:
  Teutons:
    Andreas      at Fellin
    Knud & Abel  at Wesenberg
    Yaroslav     at Pskov
  Russians:
    Domash    at Novgorod
    Vladislav at Ladoga          [2nd Ed setup; differs from 1st Ed]
 
Veche:
  One white 1VP Conquered marker, Coin x1.
 
Calendar:
  Box 1: white Victory marker.
  Box 4: black Victory marker, Levy marker, Heinrich cylinder,
         Rudolf cylinder, Karelians cylinder.
  Box 5: Andrey cylinder, Yaroslav Service marker.
  Box 6: Knud & Abel Service marker, Vladislav Service marker.
  Box 7: Aleksandr cylinder, Andreas Service marker, Domash
         Service marker.
  Box 8: Hermann cylinder.
 
Remove from Play:
  Gavrilo cylinder, mat, and Service markers.
  ("He fell in Summer 1240.")
 
Special Rules:
  None beyond standard rules.
 
Victory (2nd Ed change — replaces standard 5.3):
  At scenario end, the Teutons win only if they have AT LEAST
  7 VP AND at least DOUBLE the Russian VP total. Otherwise the
  Russians win. There is NO TIE in this scenario.

SCENARIO 5 — CRUSADE ON NOVGOROD (1240–1242)
================================================================
Span:         Up to 16 turns. Full-length scenario.
              Begin first Levy of Summer 1240 (40-Days box 1).
              Play through end of Rasputitsa 1242 (box 16).
 
Markers on Map:
  None.
 
Mustered Lords:
  Teutons:
    Hermann (Marshal) at Dorpat
    Knud & Abel       at Reval
    Yaroslav          at Odenpäh
  Russians:
    Gavrilo   at Pskov
    Vladislav at Neva
 
Veche:
  One white 1VP Conquered marker.
 
Calendar:
  Box 1: white Victory marker, Levy marker, Heinrich cylinder,
         Rudolf cylinder, Domash cylinder.
  Box 2: Yaroslav Service marker.
  Box 3: Andreas cylinder [added by 2nd Ed Changes],
         Karelians cylinder, Knud & Abel Service marker,
         Vladislav Service marker.
  Box 4: Hermann Service marker, Gavrilo Service marker.
  Box 5: Aleksandr cylinder, Andrey cylinder.
 
Remove from Play:
  No explicit removal — all 12 Lords are in play across the
  scenario span.
 
Special Rules:
  Arts of War: Do NOT remove "No Event/No Capability" cards
  (i.e., the standard 3.1.2–3.1.3 removal rule is suspended for
  this scenario; Crusade on Novgorod intentionally retains the
  three No-cards in each deck throughout play).
 
Victory:
  Standard end-of-scenario victory (5.3).
