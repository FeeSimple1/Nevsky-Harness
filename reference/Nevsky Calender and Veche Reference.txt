NEVSKY CALENDAR & VECHE — Reference
====================================
Compiled per Rules of Play (Second Edition, 2023), Sections 1.3.2,
1.3.3, 1.4.2, 2.2, 3.5.2, 4.9, and the Second Edition Changes
Summary. Where they conflict, Second Edition Rules of Play govern.
 
 
================================================================
PART I — THE CALENDAR
================================================================
 
PURPOSE  (Rule 1.3.2)
The Calendar tracks (a) time, (b) Lord Service expiry, (c) Lord
readiness to Muster, and (d) victory scores. It also displays
printed reminders for several seasonal mechanics.
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
LAYOUT
----------------------------------------------------------------
The Calendar shows EIGHT Seasons across two rows, divided into
SIXTEEN numbered 40-Days boxes (two boxes per Season). The full
range covers Summer 1240 through Rasputitsa 1242:
 
  Row 1 (1240–1241):
    Box  1– 2:  Summer 1240
    Box  3– 4:  Early Winter 1240–41
    Box  5– 6:  Late Winter 1240–41
    Box  7– 8:  Rasputitsa 1241
 
  Row 2 (1241–1242):
    Box  9–10:  Summer 1241
    Box 11–12:  Early Winter 1241–42
    Box 13–14:  Late Winter 1241–42
    Box 15–16:  Rasputitsa 1242
 
Each 40-Days box has a Levy circle, a Campaign circle, and a
Victory circle (used as described below).
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
PRINTED REMINDERS ON THE CALENDAR
----------------------------------------------------------------
The Calendar surface itself prints reminders for:
  - Command cards per side per Plan stack, by Season:
      Early Winter or Late Winter ........ 4 cards
      Rasputitsa ......................... 5 cards
      Summer ............................. 6 cards
  - Usable Transport, by Season (see 1.7.4):
      Boats:   Rasputitsa, Summer (Waterways; also Seaports for
               Ships and Novgorod for Russian Supply)
      Ships:   Rasputitsa, Summer (Seaports only)
      Sleds:   Early Winter, Late Winter (any Way)
      Carts:   Summer (Trackways only)
  - Crusade Capability auto-discard at the start of each year's
    first Late Winter 40 Days (boxes 5 and 13).
  - Ravage halving at end of each Rasputitsa (Grow, 4.9.1).
  - Cart/Sled flipping at end of Summer and end of Late Winter
    (Plow and Reap, 4.9.3).
 
The Second Edition Changes Summary added a green "Ravaged to ½
(round up)" reminder at the lower-right corner of the Calendar.
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
MARKING TIME — Levy/Campaign Marker  (Rule 2.2.2)
----------------------------------------------------------------
The round Levy/Campaign marker:
  - Sits in the current 40-Days box.
  - Shows "Levy" face up during the Levy phase (placed in the
    Levy circle).
  - Flipped to "Campaign" face up during the Campaign phase.
  - Advances to the next 40-Days box after End Campaign (4.9.5),
    flipped back to "Levy" face up.
 
When the marker advances out of the scenario's final 40 Days,
the game ends (4.9.2).
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
MARKING SERVICE — Lord Cylinders & Service Markers  (Rule 2.2.3)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Lord CYLINDERS sit on the Calendar while Lords are Ready (off
map but available to be Mustered). Cylinders enter the Calendar
either at scenario setup (per scenario) or upon Disband (3.3.2).
 
Lord SERVICE MARKERS sit on the Calendar while Lords are
Mustered (on the map). The Service marker's box is the 40 Days
in which the Lord will Disband if not Paid. Vassal Service
markers also live here ONLY if the Vassal Service Advanced Rule
(3.4.2) is in use; otherwise Vassal markers stay on Lord mats.
 
PLACEMENT ON MUSTER  (Rule 3.4.1):
  When a Lord Musters, his Service marker is placed N boxes
  ahead of the current 40-Days box, where N = his Service rating.
 
SHIFTING:
  Various Events, Capabilities, Veche actions, and end-of-card
  rules shift Service markers and cylinders left or right by a
  specified number of boxes.
    - "Right" = toward higher box numbers (more time).
    - "Left"  = toward lower box numbers (less time).
  When a direction is not specified, the player executing the
  shift chooses.
 
OFF-CALENDAR PLACEMENT:
  If a marker or cylinder would be placed or shifted past either
  end of the Calendar:
    - Left of box 1:  place just off the left edge.
    - Right of box 16: place just off the right edge.
  Further shifts in that same direction are IGNORED. The first
  shift back toward the Calendar places the marker into box 1
  (from the left) or box 16 (from the right).
 
DISBAND TRIGGER  (Rule 3.3.1):
  At the start of each Levy, every Lord whose Service marker is
  left of the current 40-Days box (i.e., his Service has run out)
  Disbands.
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
TRACKING VICTORY POINTS  (Rule 2.2.5)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Each side has a round Victory marker placed in the Victory
circle of the 40-Days box matching its current VP score:
  - Box 1 = 1 VP.
  - Box 16 = 16 VP.
  - Off the Calendar to the LEFT of box 1 = 0 VP (or ½ VP).
  - Off the Calendar to the RIGHT of box 16 = 17 VP (or 17½ VP).
 
The Victory marker has two faces:
  - Whole-number face (e.g., 5).
  - +½ face (e.g., 5½), used to mark half-points from Ravaged
    markers (5.1).
 
CAP: A side may never exceed 17½ VP — any excess is forfeit.
 
PLESKAU-SPECIFIC: In the Pleskau scenario only, additional VP
are earned for enemy Lords removed (5.1); these are tracked
with the round markers provided, on the Calendar, in addition
to the standard Victory marker.
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
END-OF-CAMPAIGN CALENDAR EVENTS  (Rule 4.9)
----------------------------------------------------------------
GROW (4.9.1):
  At the END of each Rasputitsa (turn 8 and turn 16):
  Teutons then Russians remove enemy Ravaged markers from the
  map down to half their original count (round UP).
  EXAMPLE: 5 black Ravaged on the map at end of Rasputitsa →
           reduce to 3 (5 / 2 = 2.5, round up to 3).
 
GAME END (4.9.2):
  If the concluded Campaign was the scenario's final 40 Days,
  the game ends and highest VP wins (5.3). Otherwise proceed.
 
PLOW AND REAP (4.9.3):
  At end of the second Summer 40 Days (boxes 2 and 10), all
  Lords flip all their Carts to Sleds.
  At end of the second Late Winter 40 Days (boxes 6 and 14),
  all Lords flip all their Sleds to Carts.
  Each Lord then DISCARDS Sleds or Carts down to half his
  pre-flip count (round UP).
  EXAMPLE: A Lord with 5 Carts and 0 Sleds at end of Summer
           flips to 5 Sleds, then discards down to 3 Sleds.
 
WASTAGE (4.9.4):
  Teutons then Russians: each Lord with more than one of any
  Asset type or more than one This Lord Capability card must
  discard exactly one Asset or one such card from that Lord.
  Wastage applies to LORDS only, not to the Veche box.
 
RESET (4.9.5):
  - Unstack any Lieutenants and Lower Lords.
  - Remove all Serfs from Russian mats (even if Besieged) to
    the Smerdi Capability card.
  - Discard all "This Campaign" Events.
  - Each side may discard any Arts of War cards back to its
    deck (Teutons then Russians).
  - Advance the Levy/Campaign marker to the next 40-Days box,
    flipped to Levy.
  - If the new 40 Days is the year's first Late Winter (box 5 or
    box 13), discard the Crusade Capability if in play and
    Disband the Summer Crusaders Special Vassal.
 
 
================================================================
PART II — THE NOVGOROD VECHE
================================================================
 
PURPOSE  (Rule 1.3.3, 1.4.2)
The Veche box, located at one corner of the map, holds Russian
Coin and 1VP Conquered markers representing Novgorod's wealth
and political independence. The Veche is the Russian higher
authority — analog of the Teutonic Papal Legate — and may help
Muster Lords, grant extra Levy actions, or generate VP via Call
to Arms.
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
CONTENTS AND CAPACITY  (Rule 1.4.2 Wastage)
----------------------------------------------------------------
The Veche box may hold AT MOST:
  - 8 Coin markers, AND
  - 8 white 1VP Conquered markers (i.e., 8 Veche VP).
 
Any excess earned beyond these caps is FORFEIT (lost
immediately upon being added). This is a hard ceiling on Veche
buildup.
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
SCENARIO STARTING VECHE
----------------------------------------------------------------
Each scenario specifies the Veche's starting contents:
 
  Pleskau ............... 1 white 1VP Conquered.
  Watland ............... 1 white 1VP Conquered, Coin x1.
  Return of the Prince .. 3 white 1VP Conquered, Coin x2.
  ROTP Nicolle Variant .. 3 white 1VP Conquered, Coin x2.
  Peipus ................ 4 white 1VP Conquered, Coin x3.
  Crusade on Novgorod ... 1 white 1VP Conquered.
 
Bidding for Sides (6.0): if used, the Russian player adds
1VP Conquered markers to the Veche box equal to the bid.
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
COIN INTERACTIONS WITH THE VECHE
----------------------------------------------------------------
ADDING COIN:
  Coin enters the Veche only via Events, Capabilities (e.g., the
  R8 Black Sea Trade Capability), or scenario setup. Lord Tax
  (4.7.4) adds Coin to a Lord's mat, NOT to the Veche.
 
SPENDING COIN:
  Veche Coin is NOT spent by Lords directly. Veche Coin is used
  during Veche Call to Arms (3.5.2) — see actions below — and
  via specific cards. Coin held on Lord mats is separate.
 
NOVGOROD CONQUERED  (Rule 1.4.2):
  If the Teutons Conquer Novgorod (4.5):
    - Remove ALL Coin from the Veche box.
    - If Novgorod was Sacked (Stormed and lost by Defenders),
      add the removed Coin to the Storm's Spoils (4.5.2).
    - The Veche box may continue Call to Arms even while
      Novgorod is Conquered (3.5.2).
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
VECHE CALL TO ARMS  (Rule 3.5.2)
----------------------------------------------------------------
After the Teutonic Papal Legate step (3.5.1), the Russian player
may take ONE Veche action this Levy, OR do nothing. The Veche
may act EVEN WHILE NOVGOROD IS CONQUERED.
 
The Russian player chooses ONE of the following:
 
  ACTION A — Slide a Lord cylinder LEFT
    Cost: Remove 1× 1VP Conquered marker from the Veche box.
          (Adjust the white Victory marker on the Calendar.)
    Effect: Slide one Lord cylinder on the Calendar 2 boxes
            LEFT (i.e., toward Ready / earlier Muster).
 
  ACTION B — Auto-Levy a Russian Lord
    Cost: Remove 1× 1VP Conquered marker from the Veche box.
    Effect: Automatically Levy ONE Ready Russian Lord (3.4.1)
            without rolling Fealty.
 
  ACTION C — Bonus Lordship for one Russian Lord
    Cost: Remove 1× 1VP Conquered marker from the Veche box.
    Effect: One Russian Lord at any Friendly Locale (NOT a
            Siege Locale) immediately performs an EXTRA Muster
            using his Lordship rating (3.4).
 
  ACTION D — Decline a Suzdalian Prince (gain Veche VP)
    Slide Aleksandr's and/or Andrey's cylinder(s) on the
    Calendar from Ready (at or left of current Levy) to ONE
    BOX RIGHT of the current Levy box.
    If both are Ready, the Russian must slide BOTH cylinders.
    Add ONE white 1VP Conquered marker total to the Veche box
    (subject to the 8-VP Wastage cap, 1.4.2).
 
ALEKSANDR EXCEPTION  (Rule 1.5.3, 3.5.2):
  Aleksandr has NO Fealty rating. No other Lord may Levy him.
  The Veche is the ONLY way to bring Aleksandr from Ready to
  Mustered (via Action B above, or via specific Capabilities/
  Events that add him to the map).
 
 
----------------------------------------------------------------
VECHE VP TRACKING
----------------------------------------------------------------
Veche 1VP Conquered markers count toward the Russian VP total
(5.1) just as if they were on the map:
  - Each marker in the Veche box = 1 Russian VP.
  - The white Victory marker on the Calendar reflects the
    cumulative Russian total INCLUDING Veche markers.
 
When Veche VP markers are spent (Actions A, B, or C above),
adjust the Russian Victory marker DOWN by 1 each time. When
Action D adds a marker, adjust the Russian Victory marker UP
by 1 (subject to the 8-marker Wastage cap on the Veche box,
and the 17½ cap on the Calendar).
