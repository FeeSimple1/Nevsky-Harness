NEVSKY LORDS — Mat Reference
============================
Compiled per Rules of Play (Second Edition) and Second Edition Changes.
Defaults assumed for every Lord unless noted otherwise:
  - Resource maximum: 8 each
  - Mat carries the note "(no Ships)" where applicable
  - Teutonic Lords' mats carry the note: If Ordensburgen: Commanderies
    (Commanderies count as added Seats; +1 Command when starting a
    Command card already at a Commandery — per Capability T12)
  - Russian Lords' mats carry the note: If Archbishopric: Novgorod
    (Novgorod counts as an added Seat for ALL Russian Lords; +1 Command
    when starting a Command card already at Novgorod — per Capability R15)

      marker is yet in box 1 or off the left end of the
      Calendar. Discard immediately. Models the winter
      1241–1242 pagan revolt on Ösel (Saaremaa) that Heinrich
      could not suppress alone, drawing Andreas across the
      sea ice.

      a fully-Mustered Domash with all three Novgorod Vassals
      fields 7 Militia — the largest Militia concentration on
      either side of the game, embodying Novgorod's mass urban
      foot levy that historically tipped Raven's Rock in 1242.

      Remove 3 Assets from Domash OR 3 Coin from the Veche
      (Teutons choose). Teutons may select Domash or the Veche
      even if that choice would remove fewer than three Assets
      or Coin. Discard immediately. Models the Mongols' 1238
      seizure of Torzhok, the southeastern Novgorodan stronghold
      that guarded Novgorod's grain supply route from the upper
      Volga. Domash is the Asset target specifically because his
      Vassals and office tie him to the city's urban supply
      base — squeezing the city squeezes him.

      as the natural carrier for it: "Stone Kremlin on Domash
      to harden Novgorod and Ladoga." Cross-referenced here for
      planning convenience, not because the card text names him.

  Pskov (2) .................. 2x Militia .................... Service 2

Scenario notes:
  - Crusade on Novgorod (full-length, 1240–1242):
      Gavrilo starts Mustered at Pskov.
  - Pleskau (Summer 1240):
      Gavrilo starts Mustered at Pskov; Service marker in box 4.
      Per the scenario victory conditions, this is the campaign
      where the historical Gavrilo fell defending Pskov.
  - Watland (Early Winter 1240–Rasputitsa 1241):
      Gavrilo cylinder, mat, and Service markers REMOVED from
      play (per scenario setup). The Playbook flavor text:
      "He fell in Summer 1240."
  - Peipus (Late Winter 1242–Rasputitsa 1242):
      Gavrilo cylinder begins on the Calendar at Box 13, alongside
      Heinrich, Knud & Abel, and Rudolf cylinders. The Playbook
      explicitly notes: "Gavrilo here represents rallied
      pro-Aleksandr Pskovans" — that is, the Gavrilo piece in
      Peipus is functionally a stand-in for Pskovan partisans
      who rose for Aleksandr after his liberation of the city,
      not the historical Gavrilo (who was already dead).
 
See also:
  [No Arts of War cards appear to be keyed specifically to
   Gavrilo. T7 Tverdilo (Pskovan ally) is keyed to Hermann
   and Yaroslav and concerns Pskovan dynastic politics on the
   Teutonic side, not Gavrilo. If a card keyed to Gavrilo

NOTATION RESOLUTION — "EXTRA IF [CROSS]" SEAT MECHANIC
================================================================
This block formerly held the open question on the "Extra if:"
Seat notation appearing on Aleksandr's, Andrey's, and Domash's
mats. The notation has been resolved as follows:
 
The Orthodox cross icon printed on the Russian Lord mats in the
"Extra if:" position is the Archbishopric symbol — the same
cross symbol Rule 1.3.1 establishes as marking Novgorod on the
map as the Archbishopric Seat. The mat-side notation reads:
 
    "Extra Seat if [Archbishopric in play]: [Locale]"
 
Confirmed by full scan of the Arts of War Reference document:
no Capability or Event card references Rusa, and no "Extra"-Seat
mechanism exists outside of T12 Ordensburgen (Teutonic Lords,
Commanderies) and R15 Archbishopric of Novgorod (Russian Lords,
Novgorod). The Rusa upgrade for Aleksandr and Andrey is therefore
a mat-specific rule particular to those two Lords and not tied
to any card the player Levies.
 
Three Russian Lords carry the notation:
    Aleksandr — Extra Seat if Archbishopric: Rusa
    Andrey    — Extra Seat if Archbishopric: Rusa
    Domash    — Extra Seat if Archbishopric: [no additional Locale;
                                              standard R15 only]
 
All other Russian Lords (Vladislav, Gavrilo, Karelians) receive
only the standard R15 Archbishopric default — Novgorod becomes
an Extra Seat for them when R15 is in play, with Command +1 if
they start a Command card already at Novgorod.
 
The per-Lord Mat note lines have been updated throughout the
document to reflect this resolution.
